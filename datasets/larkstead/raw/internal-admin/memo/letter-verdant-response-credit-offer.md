Thank you for the detailed data on LOT-2025-14. We have reviewed our
production records for the lot and confirm the root cause: the adhesive
cure oven ran approximately 12 degrees C low for one shift on our
production date of 2025-05-27, and the edge-stitch lamination did not
fully bond as a result. We take responsibility for this.

We accept the goods credit of 1638.30, calculated on 127 defective units
at the 12.90 invoice price, and will apply it against INV-VRD-2025-118 on
receipt of your confirmation. We understand and accept that payment on
this invoice remains held until this matter is fully resolved between us.

On the freight and receiving portion of your claim, 203.20, we are not
able to agree to this. Our supply agreement limits our liability on
defective goods to the value of the goods themselves, and does not
extend to a customer's inbound freight or receiving costs. We recognize
this is a frustrating distinction given the circumstances, and we do not
make it lightly, but our terms are clear on this point and we need to
hold to them.

We have attached a corrective action plan addressing the oven
temperature monitoring gap that allowed this shift to go undetected. As a
gesture of good faith beyond the credit, we are offering third-party
pre-shipment inspection at our own cost on the next three lots we ship
you, so you have independent verification before goods leave our
facility, not just our own quality sign-off.

We value this relationship and want LOT-2025-14 to be the exception, not
a pattern. Please let us know if the corrective action plan meets your
expectations.
