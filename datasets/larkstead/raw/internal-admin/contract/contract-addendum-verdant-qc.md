QUALITY CONTROL ADDENDUM TO SUPPLY AGREEMENT

This addendum ("Addendum") is made 2025-09-12 (the "Effective Date")
between Larkstead Goods Company, LLC ("Client") and Verdant Textiles
Co. ("Vendor", VEN-01), and amends the supply agreement between the
parties dated 2023-04-01 (the "Supply Agreement"). This Addendum was
drafted by Colefield & Tran LLP (VEN-10) following the LOT-2025-14
defect claim resolved between the parties on 2025-09-02.

1. Definitions
1.1 "Lot" means any single production and shipment batch of goods under
    the Supply Agreement, identified by a lot number.
1.2 "AQL" means Acceptable Quality Limit as defined by the applicable
    sampling standard, expressed as a percentage.
1.3 "Humidity-Cycle Test" means exposure of sample units to 85% relative
    humidity for 48 hours prior to shipment, used to detect adhesive and
    lamination defects of the kind identified in LOT-2025-14.
1.4 "Defect Rate" means the count of units failing inspection or field
    use divided by the total units in the Lot, expressed as a
    percentage.

2. Pre-shipment sampling
2.1 Vendor shall apply pre-shipment sampling at AQL 2.5, general
    inspection level II, on every Lot shipped under the Supply
    Agreement, effective from the Effective Date forward.
2.2 Vendor shall retain sampling records for each Lot and provide them
    to Client on request.

3. Humidity-cycle test
3.1 Vendor shall run the Humidity-Cycle Test on 8 sample units drawn
    from each Lot before shipment.
3.2 A Lot that fails the Humidity-Cycle Test shall not ship. Vendor
    shall notify Client of any failed Lot within 2 business days of the
    test result.

4. Third-party inspection
4.1 Vendor shall arrange third-party pre-shipment inspection, at
    Vendor's own cost, for the next 3 Lots shipped to Client following
    the Effective Date.
4.2 After those 3 Lots, third-party inspection reverts to Vendor's
    standard internal QC process unless the parties agree otherwise in
    writing.

5. Defect rate remedy
5.1 Any future Lot with a confirmed Defect Rate above 2% triggers an
    automatic credit to Client, without need for a separate claim
    process, calculated at Client's landed cost for the defective
    units, meaning the invoice price plus freight and receiving per
    unit, plus the cost of replacement freight for those units.
5.2 This remedy does not limit Client's other rights under the Supply
    Agreement for a Defect Rate at or below 2%.

6. Order of precedence
6.1 This Addendum controls over the Supply Agreement where the two
    conflict. All other terms of the Supply Agreement dated 2023-04-01
    remain in effect.
