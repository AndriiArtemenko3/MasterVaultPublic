Policy: Timekeeping and payroll calendar
Effective: 2024-02-01
Supersedes: none
Approved by: Mara Voss
Prepared by: Ana Petrova
Applies to: all hourly and salaried employees

1. Pay periods
Payroll runs semi-monthly: the 1st through the 15th, and the 16th through the last day of the month. Pay dates land on the 5th and the 20th. When either date falls on a weekend or a fixed holiday, payment moves to the prior business day instead of sliding forward.

2. Hourly employees
Hourly staff, currently the support and warehouse roles, log time daily rather than reconstructing a week from memory on Friday afternoon. Timesheets close at 23:59 on the last day of each pay period and are due to Ana by noon the next business day for the run to process on schedule; a timesheet submitted after that cutoff risks landing in the following period's run instead of the current one.

3. Salaried employees
Salaried staff do not submit hourly timesheets. They log only PTO and unpaid leave against the calendar, and a missed PTO entry does not change that pay period's amount, since salaried pay is fixed regardless of hours actually worked in a given week.

4. Correction window
Any pay discrepancy, wrong hours, a missed timesheet, an incorrect rate, must be reported to Ana within 5 business days of the pay date. Corrections reported inside that window are folded into the next scheduled run. Corrections reported later wait for the run after that, since off-cycle checks are not issued for routine errors.

5. Records
Ana records every pay period's hours and totals in Ledgerly. There is no self-serve portal; an employee who wants their own pay history simply asks Ana directly, and she pulls it within a day or two.

6. New hires starting mid-period
A hire who starts partway through a pay period is paid only for the days actually worked in that first period, prorated against the period's calendar days rather than a full half-month, and the first paycheck lands on the regular pay date for that period, not early. Hourly new hires still submit a timesheet for those partial days like everyone else.
