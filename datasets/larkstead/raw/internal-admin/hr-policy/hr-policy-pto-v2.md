Policy: Paid time off
Effective: 2026-01-01
Supersedes: 2024-01-15 version
Approved by: Mara Voss
Prepared by: Ana Petrova
Applies to: all employees; part-time staff accrue pro rata

1. Allowance
Every employee now accrues 18 days of paid time off per calendar year, up from 15 under the 2024-01-15 version, starting from day one of employment with no waiting period. Accrual runs at 1.5 days per month, so a full year of tenure banks the full 18 days by December, and a new hire who starts partway through the year still earns their first day of PTO before that same month closes. Part-time staff accrue at the same monthly rate scaled to scheduled hours against a standard 40-hour week, and Ana posts the pro-rated figure to the employee's file at hire.

2. Fixed holidays
Larkstead now observes 9 paid holidays, adding Juneteenth to the 8 already in place: New Year's Day, Presidents Day, Memorial Day, Juneteenth, Independence Day, Labor Day, Thanksgiving Day, the day after Thanksgiving, and Christmas Day. A holiday landing on a weekend shifts to the nearest weekday at Mara's discretion, and the shifted dates for the coming year go out by email each December, same as before.

3. Carryover
The carryover cap rises from 5 unused days to 6, tracking the larger annual allowance. Carried days still expire March 31, and anything unused after that date is forfeited with no cash-out option, unchanged from the prior version.

4. Requesting time off
For 1 or 2 days off, email your manager at least 3 business days ahead. For 3 or more consecutive days, give at least 10 business days of notice so the schedule can absorb the gap, especially in support and the warehouse where a missing shift is felt right away. Managers confirm or push back within 3 business days of the request, and silence past that window counts as approved, same as under the 2024 version.

5. Recordkeeping
Ana tracks balances and posts a running total to each employee once a quarter. Any employee can ask for a mid-quarter balance check by emailing her directly instead of waiting on the scheduled update, and balances already accrued under the 2024-01-15 version carry forward unchanged into this one; nobody loses banked days in the switch.
