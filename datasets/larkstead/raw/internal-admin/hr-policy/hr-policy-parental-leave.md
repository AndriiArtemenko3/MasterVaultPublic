Policy: Parental leave
Effective: 2025-09-15
Supersedes: none
Approved by: Mara Voss
Prepared by: Ana Petrova
Applies to: all employees who have completed one year of tenure at the start of leave; part-time staff eligible on the same tenure basis, paid at their regular pro-rated rate; contractors are not covered, consistent with the contractor engagement policy

1. Eligibility
Any employee who has completed a full year of continuous employment as of the first day of leave qualifies for paid parental leave following the birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child. An employee who has not yet reached the one-year mark may still take the leave under the standard PTO and unpaid-leave provisions, but the paid parental benefit itself does not start early. Tenure is counted from the hire date on file, not the offer date.

2. Duration and pay
Eligible employees receive 8 weeks of paid leave at full base pay, or at the pro-rated rate for part-time staff, calculated against their scheduled hours in the 3 months before leave begins. The 8 weeks may be taken continuously starting at or near the birth or placement date, or split into two blocks within the 12 months following that date, with Mara's sign-off on the split arrangement. Leave beyond the 8 weeks runs against accrued PTO or unpaid leave at the employee's choice.

3. Phase-back schedule
Returning employees are not required to jump straight back to a full week. Weeks 1 and 2 of the return run at 50% of the employee's normal scheduled hours, weeks 3 and 4 run at 75%, and full scheduled hours resume in week 5. Pay during the phase-back period matches the actual hours worked plus PTO drawn down to cover the gap, if the employee chooses to top up rather than work reduced hours at reduced pay. An employee may skip the phase-back and return to full hours immediately if they prefer; the schedule is an option, not a requirement.

4. Notice
Employees give at least 30 days of advance notice where the timing is known, as with an adoption placement date or a due date well ahead of delivery. Where that much notice is not possible, notice as soon as the date is known is enough. Notice goes to the employee's manager and to Ana so coverage and payroll can be arranged before the leave starts.

5. Job protection and benefits
The employee's role, pay rate, and any standing benefits continue unchanged through the leave and on return; there is no reduction in title or compensation tied to having taken parental leave. Health coverage, where applicable, continues without interruption during the paid 8 weeks.
