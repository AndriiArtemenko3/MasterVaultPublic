Policy: Performance review cycle
Effective: 2025-11-03
Supersedes: none
Approved by: Mara Voss
Prepared by: Ana Petrova
Applies to: all employees; part-time staff reviewed on the same calendar with scope adjusted to scheduled hours; contractors are not covered

1. Annual review
Every employee gets a formal review in January, covering the prior calendar year. The first cycle under this policy runs in January 2026 and covers calendar 2025. The review is a 30 to 45 minute conversation with the employee's manager, or with Mara directly for anyone without a separate manager, and it goes over what worked, what didn't, and where the role is headed next.

2. Mid-year check-in
A shorter check-in happens in July, roughly six months off the January cycle. This is a progress conversation, not a scored review, and no written rating comes out of it. Its purpose is to catch a drifting goal or a workload problem before it turns into a surprise at the January review, so nobody hears something new about their standing for the first time in January.

3. No forced ranking
Larkstead does not rank employees against each other and does not fit reviews to a predetermined distribution of scores. Each review is judged against that role's own expectations, not against how the rest of the team happened to perform that year. Two people on the same team can both receive a strong review in the same cycle if both earned it, and neither manager is asked to hold anyone back to keep a curve.

4. Documentation
Ahead of the January conversation, the employee writes a short self-summary and the manager writes a short summary independently, without reading each other's notes first, and both go to Ana for the employee's file once the conversation has happened. There is no fixed form. A page is plenty; nobody needs to pad it out.

5. New hires
An employee hired after the mid-year check-in gets a 90-day check-in instead, timed to their own start date rather than the calendar, and then joins the standard cycle at the next January review that falls at least 90 days after that first check-in.

6. Compensation
Raises are considered at the January review but are not automatic and are not tied to a fixed percentage. Mara makes the final call on any compensation change coming out of a review, in consultation with the employee's manager where one exists.
