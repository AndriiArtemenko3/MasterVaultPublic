Policy: Anti-harassment and complaint path
Effective: 2025-01-13
Supersedes: none
Approved by: Mara Voss
Prepared by: Ana Petrova
Applies to: all employees, full and part time, and contractors while performing Larkstead work, including remote and on-site staff

1. Policy statement
Larkstead prohibits harassment, discrimination, and retaliation based on any protected characteristic, in the office, in the field, in the Portland backroom, or in any Larkstead-related chat, email thread, or off-site event. This applies to conduct between coworkers, and to conduct involving customers, vendors, or contractors that an employee encounters while doing Larkstead work.

2. Standard reporting path
An employee who experiences or witnesses harassment reports it directly to Mara Voss or Ana Petrova, whichever person the employee is more comfortable approaching. There is no other HR contact; Larkstead's headcount does not support a separate HR function. Reports can be made in person, by phone, or by email, and email is preferred because it timestamps the report automatically.

3. Conflict-of-interest path
If the complaint involves Mara, involves Ana, or the employee does not want to go through either of them for any reason, the employee may contact Larkstead's outside counsel, Colefield & Tran LLP, directly. Colefield & Tran takes the intake, keeps it separate from whichever of Mara or Ana is named, and routes the fact-finding to the other one, or handles it entirely outside the company if both are named in the complaint.

4. Response timeline
Whoever receives the report, Mara, Ana, or Colefield & Tran, acknowledges it within 2 business days. A fact-finding review follows and normally closes within 15 business days, longer only if a witness is out and cannot be reached sooner. The reporting employee gets an outcome summary in writing once it closes.

5. Non-retaliation
No employee faces a negative consequence, formal or informal, for reporting in good faith or for taking part in an investigation. A manager who retaliates against a reporting employee is subject to the same process as any other harassment complaint, no matter their title.

6. Confidentiality
Details of a complaint go only to the people who need them to investigate and resolve it. Everyone else stays out of it.
