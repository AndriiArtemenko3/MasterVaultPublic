Competitor: ErgoNest
Card revised: 2024-11-06 by TA
Prior revision: 2024-08-19
Sources: ergonest site checked 2024-11-04; a showroom walk-in on 2024-10-29 who'd spent a week comparing their chair lineup before coming to us.

## Positioning

Direct-import reseller of white-label ergonomic desks and chairs, still selling on price and shipping speed. The August note flagged the parts shelf as our strongest angle. This revision digs into the chair side specifically, since on paper that's where their catalog looks biggest.

## Price posture

No change from last cycle. Still tracking 10-18% under our list, with weekly discount codes per the site check.

## Where they win

- 9 chair models listed against our 1, graphite black or fog gray on the Rowan
- the walk-in said the range felt like more choice at first glance: mesh back, a leather look, three headrest styles
- lower sticker on most of those 9, though not all. Two of their models actually price above the Rowan once you match the spec sheet feature for feature

## Where we win

- one platform means one part list. Every Rowan chair takes the same 24.00 caster set and 29.00 armrest pads no matter which of their nine she'd almost bought instead, and ErgoNest likely runs nine different part numbers behind that range, none of them published anywhere she could find
- she couldn't get a straight answer on parts for the model she almost bought. Three tabs deep on their site and still no materials list
- a smaller catalog is easier for support to know cold

## Ground rules

Frame this as depth versus breadth, not just cheaper versus pricier. Nothing customer-facing names them. Keep it at "another direct-import option."
