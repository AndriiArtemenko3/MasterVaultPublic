Analysis: bundle unit sales, Fledgling vs Canopy since launch
Date: 2025-04-01
Author: Tom Aldridge
Data: combined Pipewell and Shopstack export pulled 2025-03-31; both bundles
counted from their shared 2024-07-01 launch through the pull date.

## Question

Nine months into both bundles, which one is actually moving, and where?

## Table

| Bundle | Units total | DTC units | B2B units |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fledgling workstation | 41 | 39 | 2 |
| Canopy team (per seat) | 17 | 5 | 12 |

## Findings

1. Fledgling wins on raw units, 41 to 17, which tracks: it's the cheaper
   single-desk bundle and most of its buyers are one person furnishing one
   office, not a company furnishing twelve.
2. Canopy wins the B2B column outright, 12 to 2, and it isn't close.
   Stonebridge Accounting's 12-seat close on 2025-03-05 is the whole reason:
   12 desks, 12 chairs, 12 single arms, one Sparrow kit each, 1,099.00 list
   per seat, 13,188.00 total, no discount because 12 seats sits well under
   the 25-unit tier floor.
3. The 2 B2B Fledgling units are unrelated single desks bought ahead of a
   bigger decision at two different small accounts. Not a multi-seat close,
   just early footholds.
4. List revenue since launch: 36,859.00 on Fledgling, 18,683.00 on Canopy,
   55,542.00 combined. That's against 1,134,900.00 in total 2024 revenue, so
   nine months of two bundles is still a small slice of the business, not
   the main event.
5. Canopy prices 200.00 higher per seat than Fledgling, and that gap is
   mostly the Heron single monitor arm sitting in the Canopy component list
   that Fledgling doesn't carry. The margin story and the unit story aren't
   the same story here.

## Caveats

One account is doing all the work in the Canopy column. Pull Stonebridge out
and B2B Canopy drops to zero. This is not yet a trend. It's one closed deal
carrying a whole column on its back, and I'd rather flag that now than have
someone quote a 12-to-2 split as a pattern in Q3. The window also blends a
partial 2024 (six months post-launch) with a partial 2025 (three months), so
don't annualize either total without splitting the two years apart first.
The Roost executive bundle isn't in this table at all. It launched
2025-01-20 and at ten weeks old doesn't have enough of a sales history to
sit next to two nine-month-old bundles without skewing the comparison.
