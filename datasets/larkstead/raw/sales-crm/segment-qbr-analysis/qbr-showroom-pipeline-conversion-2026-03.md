## Question

How many showroom walk-ins turn into a real opportunity, and is Kestrel actually representative or a lucky one-off?

## Table

| Year | Walk-ins logged (sign-in sheet) | Became a Pipewell opportunity |
|---|---|---|
| 2024 | 118 | 0 |
| 2025 | 146 | 1 (Kestrel, November) |
| 2026 YTD, through Feb | 22 | 0 |

## Findings

1. 286 walk-ins across a little over two years, one opportunity. That's not a channel yet, it's a lottery ticket, and I don't want to keep telling Mara the showroom is a pipeline source until there's a second data point sitting next to Kestrel.
2. Grace Liu's visit stuck for a reason that has nothing to do with foot traffic: Yuki followed up the same week and had three chairs pulled and waiting when Kestrel's team came back for a second look, per the recap she sent after that December visit. Speed, not the showroom itself, might be the actual variable worth testing.
3. Kestrel is still in discovery four months later, weighing our list price against an ErgoNest lease quote. "Converted" is generous language for an opportunity that hasn't closed and has no signed date on the calendar; PW-kestrel-10seat could still go either way.
4. The sign-in sheet undercounts on its own terms. People wander in, look at the floor chair, and leave without writing anything down, and there's no putting a real number on that leak with a paper form.
5. Walk-in volume isn't flat across the year either. The 2025 total of 146 clusters harder in September and October than any other two months, which lines up with fall budget planning at small offices more than with anything we changed in the showroom itself. Worth remembering before anyone credits a display refresh for a seasonal bump.

## Caveats

The walk-in counts come from a sheet at the front desk, not a system, so double entries and skipped days are both plausible and I haven't audited either one. n=1 for the actual conversion, which means finding 1 is a case study, not a rate, and shouldn't be forecast against. The September-October clustering in finding 5 is two data points from one year, not a pattern yet either.
