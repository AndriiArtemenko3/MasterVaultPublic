## Findings

1. wfh-professional is the only segment with two names on the roster, Nora
   Feld and Marcus Bell, onboarded three weeks apart back in early 2024. Both
   still show as active in the mailing list, no unsubscribes on either.
2. Every other segment ties at one. "Largest" right now just means two beats
   one, nothing more, and I'd rather say that plainly than dress it up as a trend.
3. Tessa Nakamura, the lone accessibility name, has filed two service tickets
   since her June order and both closed same-day with a top rating. No other
   single-name segment can say that.
4. Small sample, real signal. The smallest segment isn't the unhappiest one,
   which matters before anyone reweights spend toward the bigger groups on
   headcount alone.
5. Spread across the calendar year is close to even, one first order roughly
   every six to eight weeks from January through December. Nothing clusters
   around a single campaign month, so whatever's driving acquisition isn't a
   one-time push.
6. Ruben Silva, the grad-student name, is the only customer here who opened
   with the smallest possible basket, a single Wren laptop stand. Everyone
   else in this table opened with a desk or a chair attached.

## Caveats

n=8. One more name in any row flips the leaderboard, and a single unhappy
ticket from Nora Feld or Marcus Bell would erase the wfh-professional lead
entirely since it only holds by one name. Alma Reyes's first order lands
2025-02-06, two days after this pull, so she's absent here on purpose, not by
oversight, and Kit Donovan, Beatrix Mwangi, and Sam Whitfield haven't ordered
at all yet as of this pull. This table describes eight names, not the
customer base.
