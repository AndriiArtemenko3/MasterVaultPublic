## Findings

1. B2B closed the quarter at 35% of revenue, matching the full-year number Mara's been asking about since January. First quarter we've hit the target itself rather than the run rate toward it, and this time it's Ana's Ledgerly close behind the figure, not a back-of-envelope guess.
2. Bluebird Therapy Collective signed 2026-02-12 at 9 seats, paid list, no tier applied because nine seats sits under every discount threshold we have. Ruth Amadi told me the parts catalog and the Finch footrest depth mattered more to her than a percentage off, which tracks with the read on healthcare I flagged last quarter.
3. Two accounts stalled in negotiation instead of closing. Beacon Legal, 18 seats, wants warranty language settled in the MSA before Marcus Hale signs anything. Nordlicht Games, 30 seats, is still working through a discount number that isn't the one I first quoted back in January, and Felix Braun hasn't pushed back on the correction yet, which either means he didn't notice or he's saving it for the next call.
4. The churn-risk board holds at two names, same as last quarter: Stonebridge, cooling since the mat-defect replacements shipped last summer, and Foxglove, weighing an ErgoNest quote against a renewal decision that isn't due until August. Different root causes. I keep saying don't average them because someone always tries to average them anyway.
5. Harborview Architects opened a new conversation this month too, an 8-seat expansion on top of their original 22, PW-harborview-expansion. Elena Brandt reached out unprompted, which doesn't happen often on an account we haven't touched since the original 2024 close, so I'm treating it as a real signal rather than Q1 padding.

## Caveats

n=12. The 35% figure is a full quarter close, but Bluebird landing at list price and the smallest seat count on the books this quarter flatters average deal size a little, so don't extrapolate a typical B2B ticket size from one data point. Harborview's expansion is a discovery conversation, not a number, and I've left it out of the table above for that reason.
