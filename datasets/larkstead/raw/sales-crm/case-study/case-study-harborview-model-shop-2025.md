Case study: Harborview Architects
Published: 2025-01-24
Written by: Sofia Grieg, approved by Elena Brandt 2025-01-20

## Who

Harborview Architects runs two studio floors in Portland, drafting and model work for mid-size commercial projects. Desks needed to hold steady at full height when a project team gathers around one to review a drawing, and the shared model-shop stations get used by whoever is on review duty that day rather than one fixed owner. Elena Brandt, who runs studio operations, spent months checking the standing desks against the studio's existing 44 in drafting tables before she signed.

## What they bought

22 seats of the Canopy team bundle across both floors: 60 in Birch standing desk, Rowan task chair, one Heron single monitor arm, and a Sparrow cable kit per seat. Six of the desks in the shared model shop carry a Heron dual monitor arm instead of the single, ordered on its own invoice once Elena's finance team asked for the model-shop hardware tracked separately from the rest of the order.

## How it went

The main order landed across two dock windows in October 2024, split because the building's freight elevator only fits one pallet at a time, so Dmitri's team staged deliveries floor by floor rather than all at once. The six dual arms for the model shop followed in December on their own PO, installed room by room without closing the shop down for a single day. No change was made to the chair color or desk configuration already delivered upstairs.

## Results

Zero stability complaints across five months of daily model-shop reviews, some running six people deep around one desk at full height. No wobble, no callback. Both October dock windows landed on schedule with no missed slot, and the December arm install didn't hold up a single desk already in use. One parts catalog now covers standing desks on two floors instead of two separate vendor relationships, which matters more to Elena's finance team than it sounds.

> "We push these to full height for every review, sometimes six people leaning on one edge to look at a drawing. They haven't budged once."
> Elena Brandt, studio operations, Harborview Architects
