Case study: Petrel Analytics
Published: 2025-11-18
Written by: Sofia Grieg, approved by Sanjay Iyer 2025-11-10

## Who

Petrel Analytics is a data consultancy with no central office. All fifteen people on the team work from home, spread across separate addresses in different states, and head of operations Sanjay Iyer wanted no one running a lesser setup than a teammate two states over just because hiring landed them in different places. No storefront visit, no showroom pickup, nothing but a form and a shipping label for every single hire.

## What they bought

Fifteen Fledgling workstation bundles, one per hire: 48 in Birch standing desk, Rowan task chair in graphite, and a Sparrow cable kit. Every unit shipped on the same configuration, home office or not, and each desk's height-adjustment memory was set to the same standard preset before it left the warehouse rather than left blank for the new hire to figure out.

## How it went

Sanjay circulated an address-collection form to the team in August 2025 and returned it within the month. Thirteen confirmed addresses went out as the first wave in September, each shipped on its own rather than consolidated onto one dock, since a fifteen-address rollout doesn't work like a normal office delivery. Account executive Yuki Tanaka tracked every address against the form individually and confirmed each shipment in writing as it went out, so nobody on Sanjay's team was left guessing whether their unit had shipped yet. The last two units followed in October once those final two hires started, each one going out the day its address was confirmed.

## Results

All fifteen addresses ended up on the identical bundle, with zero units misrouted across thirteen separate deliveries in the first wave. The final two arrived within a month of their hires starting, closing out the order without anyone waiting past their start date for a desk. One standard configuration now covers the entire team regardless of where a hire happens to live. No exceptions.

> "Nobody on this team shares an office, so nobody should end up with the leftover desk. Every home setup matches, down to the cable kit."
> Sanjay Iyer, head of operations, Petrel Analytics
