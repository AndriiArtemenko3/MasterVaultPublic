Case study: Cobalt Dental Group
Published: 2026-03-10
Written by: Sofia Grieg, approved by Sam Ortiz, 2026-03-06

## Who

Cobalt Dental Group runs four clinics around the Portland area, in Beaverton, Hillsboro, and Tigard. Forty seats split between front-office and admin staff and clinician charting stations. The group had been sitting on nine-year-old, mismatched fixed-height desks picked up piecemeal across three office moves, and admin complaints about the furniture were frequent enough that office manager Sam Ortiz started the search herself.

## What they bought

40 seats of the Canopy team bundle: 60 in Birch standing desk, Rowan task chair in graphite black, Heron single monitor arm, Sparrow cable kit. Two seats, in windowless back offices, added a Vireo smart desk lamp for staff who had been working without natural light all day. The order qualified for Larkstead's B2B volume pricing; no percentages or contract total are published here.

## How it went

Sam's inquiry came in 2025-06-24, through the site's B2B form. The deal closed 2025-11-14, a five-month cycle that ran through a full budget cycle on Cobalt's side and a competing quote from another supplier. Delivery ran in four waves, one per clinic, so no single location lost its front desk and charting stations on the same day. The first wave landed 2025-12-10 and the last on 2026-02-18. Every wave hit its scheduled date; none slipped, and none of the four clinics went a day without a working front desk during the changeover.

## Results

Four clinics, one furniture standard, live as of 2026-02-18. Zero of the four waves missed its date. Every seat in the group now draws from the same parts shelf, so a worn caster or a loose armrest pad is a small parts order, not a chair replacement. Warranty and support routing are identical across all four locations, so staff moving between clinics never have to relearn who to call.

> "We looked at a cheaper import quote too. What settled it for me was the parts shelf. Four clinics, one furniture standard: when something wears out, we order the part instead of another desk."
> Sam Ortiz, office manager, Cobalt Dental Group
