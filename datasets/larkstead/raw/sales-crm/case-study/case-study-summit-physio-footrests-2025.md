Case study: Summit Physio Group
Published: 2025-06-18
Written by: Sofia Grieg, approved by Omar Haddad 2025-06-12

## Who

Summit Physio Group opened a new Oregon location in November 2024, eight treatment rooms staffed by therapists who rotate between rooms across a shift rather than owning one room each. Every room already had rolling stools from the practice's prior setup, so the real constraint was a desk and a footrest that could survive a disinfectant wipe-down between every single patient without clouding or absorbing liquid the way the practice's previous vendor's tables did after repeated wipe-downs. Small clinic, particular needs.

## What they bought

Eight 48 in Birch standing desks, one per treatment room, each set with lift-height presets so staff moving between rooms across a shift don't have to reset anything by hand. Eight Finch footrests went in alongside them for patients doing balance work between exam tables. No chairs: Omar kept the practice's existing rolling stools in every room, and nothing about that changed.

## How it went

The order shipped as one LTL delivery in early November 2024, timed to land the same week as the new location's exam-table delivery so the room build-out finished in a single pass. Nothing about the rollout needed a second trip, and every desk's laminate and every footrest's vinyl skin have held up through daily wipe-downs since.

## Results

Zero desks or footrests replaced as a whole unit since the November 2024 install. Footrest reorders have become the account's steady rhythm instead: four separate orders since opening, each for the full set of eight, the most recent landing in May 2025. Patients lean on that foam harder and more often than Omar expected walking in, and Larkstead ships the part, not a new desk, every time one wears through. No new SKUs, no escalation, no lost time chasing a quote each round. Eight rooms, one recurring line item, same as the first time.

> "Patients push through a whole session of balance work on that footrest. We don't think about the furniture anymore, we just order the part when it wears out."
> Omar Haddad, owner, Summit Physio Group
