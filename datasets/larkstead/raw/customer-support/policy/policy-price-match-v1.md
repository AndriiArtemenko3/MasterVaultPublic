## 1. Policy statement

Larkstead does not price match against any competitor, retailer, or marketplace listing. The price shown on an order at checkout is the price the customer pays, and no agent may adjust it to match an outside quote.

## 2. Why this policy exists

Larkstead's catalog is small by design: one desk platform, one chair, and a parts shelf deep enough that a five-year-old chair gets repaired instead of replaced. Holding that parts shelf costs money, and the cost is folded into the desk and chair price rather than sold back to the customer as an add-on later. A competing desk at a lower sticker price is very often a desk with no replacement motor, no replacement leveling feet, and no path back to a support queue when something breaks. Matching that price would mean matching the stripped-down offer sitting behind it, and Larkstead is not willing to make that trade on a customer's behalf.

The standard one-year warranty, the Portland showroom, and a support agent who has actually assembled the desk in question are part of the price too. None of that appears on a spec sheet next to the dollar figure, and a side-by-side price comparison misses it every time.

## 3. What to tell a customer who asks

Agents should not offer a discount, a coupon, or a one-time exception when a customer cites a lower price found elsewhere. State plainly that Larkstead does not match outside pricing, and that the reason is the parts shelf and the service behind the number, not the number itself. If a customer continues to push, escalate the ticket to Priya Raman rather than negotiating inside it.
