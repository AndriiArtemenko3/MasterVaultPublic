## Why cables need spare length on a standing desk

Q: Why do my cables pull tight when the desk goes up?
A: Any standing desk with a moving frame needs extra cable length built in, because the distance from your outlet or hub to the desktop changes across the full height range, roughly 24.5 to 50 inches on a Birch desk. If your cables are cut tight for sitting height, raising the desk yanks on every connector at the back of your monitor or dock.

Q: How much extra length should I leave?
A: Loop enough cable to reach the desk at its highest setting with a few inches to spare, then gather the excess with the Magpie tray or an Aspen sleeve rather than letting it hang loose.

## Using the Magpie tray

Q: Where does the Magpie tray (LS-ACC-003) mount?
A: It clamps to the underside of the desktop, usually centered behind the monitor position, and travels with the desk since it's attached to the surface rather than the frame. Power strips, a dock, and the coiled extra cable all sit inside it, off the floor.

Q: Does the tray get in the way at the desk's lowest height?
A: Not on a 48 or 60 in Birch desk. On the 72 in desk, mount the tray a few inches back from center if you sit close to the edge, since the extra desktop depth means a centered tray can brush a tall user's knees at the lowest setting.

## Using the Aspen sleeves

Q: What are the Aspen felt sleeves (LS-ACC-004) for?
A: They bundle 3 to 6 loose cables into one soft tube so power and data lines don't tangle with each other on their way from the tray to your devices. The 3-pack covers most single-monitor setups with room left over for a lamp cord.

Q: Tray, sleeves, or both?
A: Both, if you can. The tray handles the bulk and the spare-length loop, the sleeves keep the runs to your monitor and laptop looking like one cable instead of five tangled together, and most desks with a dock, a monitor arm, and a desk lamp end up needing at least two sleeves plus the tray to stay tidy through a full week of sitting and standing.

Q: Do I need either one for a basic single-monitor setup?
A: Not strictly. A single sleeve is often enough.
