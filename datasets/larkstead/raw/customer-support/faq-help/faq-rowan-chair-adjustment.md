## Seat height

Q: How do I change the seat height?
A: Lift the paddle lever under the front right of the seat while you shift your weight up or down; the gas cylinder responds to your weight, not to how hard you pull the lever. Let go once you're at the height you want and the seat locks there.

Q: What height should I set it to?
A: Sit down and check that your feet rest flat on the floor with your knees at roughly a right angle. If your elbows sit noticeably below desk height once your feet are flat, raise the desk rather than the chair.

## Tilt tension

Q: How do I stop the chair from tipping back too easily?
A: Turn the round knob under the seat, on the left side, clockwise to add resistance and counterclockwise to loosen it. A few turns makes a bigger difference than it feels like at the time, so adjust in small steps and test the recline after each one.

Q: Can I lock the chair upright so it doesn't recline at all?
A: Yes. The small toggle lever next to the tilt knob locks the seat upright; flip it back to center to unlock full recline again.

## Armrests

Q: My armrests won't move. Are they adjustable?
A: Height only, not width or pivot. Press the button on the inside of each armrest post and slide up or down at the same time; the two sides move independently, so check that both sit level once you're done.

Q: The armrest slid down on its own overnight. Is that normal?
A: No, and it usually means the height-lock pin inside the post has worn or wasn't seated properly last time you adjusted it. Press the button fully in, slide the armrest to the very top, then release the button before lowering it again to reset the pin.

Q: Do the armrests come off for cleaning?
A: Not without tools. If you need them removed for a deep clean or repair, that's a job for support rather than something to try loose in the living room.
