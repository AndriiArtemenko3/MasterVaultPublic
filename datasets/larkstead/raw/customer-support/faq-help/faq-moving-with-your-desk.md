## Before you start taking it apart

Q: What should I do before I touch a single bolt?
A: Power the desk down fully at the outlet and take a phone photo of the current cable routing before you unplug anything. That photo saves real time later when you're trying to remember which cable ran through the Magpie tray and which one looped over the crossbar.

Q: Should I lower the desk first?
A: Yes, all the way down. A desk at full height is heavier at the top and much easier to tip while you're working underneath it than one sitting at its lowest setting.

## Taking it apart

Q: What order should I disassemble the desk in?
A: Remove anything mounted to the desktop first, monitor arms, the Magpie tray, a desk lamp clamp, before you touch the frame itself. From there, unplug the control box, disconnect the leg cables, then remove the crossbar bolts, and only then separate the legs from the top. Doing it in reverse, legs first, puts the full weight of the desktop on whatever bolts you haven't gotten to yet.

Q: How do I bag the hardware so I don't lose anything?
A: One bag per leg, taped directly to that leg so it travels with the part it belongs to rather than riding loose in a box with everything else. Label each bag left or right if your desk has asymmetric leg hardware; most Birch frames don't, but it takes ten seconds to check.

## Putting it back together

Q: Does the desk need to be recalibrated after a move?
A: Almost always, yes. Disconnecting the motor cables resets the desk's memory of its own travel range, so recalibrate before you try the memory height buttons again: hold the down arrow until the desk lowers fully and the display flashes.

Q: How do I re-level it on a new floor?
A: Check each leg with a small level once the frame is bolted back together and standing on its own. Uneven floors are common enough that every Birch desk ships with a leveling feet set built into the base, and a quarter turn on the low corner's foot is usually enough; order a replacement set, LS-ACC-011, only if a foot is cracked or missing rather than just worn down.

Q: What if the desk still rocks after leveling?
A: Check the crossbar bolts before blaming the floor. A frame that wasn't torqued in the crossing pattern during reassembly rocks no matter how well the feet are adjusted.
