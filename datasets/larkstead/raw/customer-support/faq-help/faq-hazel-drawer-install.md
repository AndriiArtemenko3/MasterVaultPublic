## Checking clearance

Q: How much clearance do I need under the desktop?
A: At least 3.5 inches between the underside of your desktop and whatever sits directly below it, your knees, a keyboard tray, or another accessory, since the drawer body itself is 2.75 inches tall and needs a little room to slide without dragging. Measure at the spot you plan to mount it, not just at the front edge, because some desks taper slightly toward the back.

Q: Will it fit under a desk with a keyboard tray already installed?
A: Usually not in the same spot. Mount the drawer to one side of the tray rather than directly above it, or move it further back under the desktop where the tray's rails don't reach.

Q: Does it work on any Larkstead desk?
A: It's built for the Birch desk's flat underside and mounts to the birch veneer without trouble. A desk with a mesh or perforated underside won't hold the mounting screws as securely.

## Mounting the rails

Q: How do the mounting rails attach?
A: Two steel rails screw directly into the underside of the desktop using the wood screws in the box, four per rail. On a stock Birch desktop you don't need pilot holes; the screws bite into the veneer cleanly on their own.

Q: Do I need to drill new holes, or does it use existing ones?
A: New holes. The Hazel drawer doesn't share mounting points with any other Larkstead accessory, so plan the position before you drive the first screw, and dry-fit the rails first if you're not confident about spacing.

Q: What tools do I need?
A: A Phillips screwdriver and a tape measure. That's it.

## Weight limit

Q: How much weight can the drawer hold?
A: 10 pounds, evenly loaded. That covers a stack of notebooks, a small tray of pens and cables, or a laptop charger and a few cords, which is what most people actually keep in it.

Q: Does the limit change if I mount it off-center?
A: Yes, lower it in your head if you do. Mounting it off to one side rather than centered under the desktop puts more twisting force on the two rails closest to the load, so treat 10 pounds as the centered maximum and back off closer to 7 or 8 pounds for a corner mount.
