## Everyday cleaning

Q: How do I clean coffee or water off the mat?
A: Blot it right away with a dry cloth or paper towel rather than wiping, which just spreads the spill toward the edges. For anything that's already dried, a cloth dampened with plain water and a little mild dish soap lifts most everyday spills without leaving a ring.

Q: Can I machine wash it?
A: No. The Alder mat has a stitched edge and a non-slip backing that don't hold up to a washing machine or dryer, so hand cleaning on the surface is the only method we recommend.

## Stains

Q: I have an ink or marker stain. What removes it?
A: Dab, don't rub, with a cloth lightly dampened in isopropyl alcohol, working from the outside of the stain inward so it doesn't spread further. Test on a hidden corner first, since the charcoal and sage colors show fading faster than sand under repeated alcohol cleaning.

Q: What about grease or makeup?
A: Mild dish soap and warm water on a soft cloth, worked in gently, handles most grease and makeup marks. Rinse the soap out with a barely damp cloth afterward so residue doesn't attract more dust.

## What to avoid

Q: What should I never use on the mat?
A: Bleach, acetone, and any solvent-based cleaner will break down the surface coating and can discolor it permanently within a single use. Skip the dishwasher and the dryer too; heat warps the backing.

Q: Is it okay to leave the mat rolled up in storage?
A: Short term, yes. Rolled for more than a few weeks, the surface can develop a crease that doesn't always flatten back out, so lay it flat under something heavy for a day after long storage before using it again.
