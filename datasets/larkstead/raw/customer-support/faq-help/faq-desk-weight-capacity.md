## Desktop weight ratings

Q: How much weight can a Birch standing desk hold?
A: 220 lbs on the 48 in desk, 200 lbs on the 60 in, and 180 lbs on the 72 in, measured as the total load sitting on the desktop at once: monitors, arms, a laptop, everything.

Q: Why does the rating drop as the desk gets longer?
A: Longer desktops put more leverage on the same lifting columns, so the frame derates the longer models to hold the same safety margin as the 48 in desk. It's the same motor and column set across all three lengths, just spread further apart.

Q: Does the rating change with height?
A: Yes. Those numbers are the rating at the desk's lowest quarter of its height range. Fully raised, near the 50 in mark, plan on roughly 15% less capacity, since the columns are doing more work at full extension.

## Monitor arm clamp placement

Q: Where's the strongest point to clamp a monitor arm?
A: Directly over a frame rail. The Birch frame has a stabilizer bar running under the desktop about 3 inches in from the back edge, and a clamp seated within an inch or two of that bar transfers load into the frame instead of into the desktop material alone.

Q: What happens if I clamp somewhere else, like the front edge?
A: The front edge of the desktop isn't reinforced the same way, so a clamp out there is bearing on wood and laminate rather than metal, which is fine for a light single arm but risks a slow sag over months with a heavier dual arm loaded up with two large monitors.

Q: How do I find the stabilizer bar if I can't see it?
A: Run a hand along the underside about 3 inches from the back edge. You'll feel a raised metal strip running the width of the desk. That's your target.

Q: Can I clamp two monitor arms on the same desk?
A: Yes, as long as each clamp lands on or near the stabilizer bar and the combined desktop load, arms plus monitors plus whatever else is sitting up there, stays under the desk's rated total.
