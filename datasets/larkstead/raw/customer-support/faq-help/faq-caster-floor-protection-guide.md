## Which caster fits your floor

Q: Does the Rowan chair come with the right wheels for my floor already?
A: It ships with a harder wheel meant for carpet and low-pile rugs. If your workspace floor is hardwood, laminate, tile, or stone, that stock wheel will roll fine but over months of pivoting in one spot it can leave faint scuffing, so most customers on hard flooring swap in the soft caster set.

Q: What's the soft caster set and how many wheels are in it?
A: LS-ACC-008, five soft wheels, one for each point of the Rowan base's five-point spider. Replace all five together rather than mixing hard and soft, an uneven set rolls unevenly and wears the odd one out faster than the rest.

Q: Any floor it's not good for?
A: Very soft or high-pile carpet. The soft wheel sinks in a little and rolling gets sluggish, that's really a job for the wheel it already shipped with.

## Swapping them

Q: How do I actually change the casters?
A: Tip the chair back or flip it seat-down on something soft, grip the wheel where it meets the leg, and pull straight out, no tool needed for most units. If a wheel is stiff, wrap it in a cloth and use pliers rather than gripping bare metal, then press the new caster in until it seats with a light click.

Q: How long does the whole swap take?
A: Five wheels, maybe ten minutes total once you're set up, most of that is just getting the chair into a stable position to work on.

## Do you still need a mat underneath?

Q: If I put on soft casters, do I still need a floor mat?
A: For hardwood especially, yes, we'd still recommend one. Soft wheels cut down on scuffing, they don't eliminate the fine grit and dust that gets ground into a floor finish under a chair that pivots in the same square foot all day. A low-pile chair mat sized to clear your full swivel radius, roughly 45 by 53 inches under a 60 in desk or 48 by 60 inches under the 72 in desk, handles that the rest of the way.

Q: Can I just use an Alder desk mat on the floor instead of buying a separate chair mat?
A: No. The Alder mat is a desktop surface, fabric top over a cork backing, and it's not built to take rolling chair weight or foot traffic. Used on the floor it tears at the edges within weeks. A dedicated chair mat is a different product built for that load.
