## Reading the display

Q: Where do I see an error code?
A: The small LED display on the control pad normally just shows your current height in inches. When something's wrong it switches to a two-character code instead, E followed by a number, and stays there until the fault clears or you power-cycle the desk.

Q: Does the desk still work while a code is showing?
A: Sometimes. E01 and E02 usually still let you drive the desk manually. E03 and E04 tend to lock the motors out until the fault is addressed.

## What each code means

Q: What does E01 mean?
A: E01 is an overload or obstruction stop. The desk detected resistance while moving, something under the desktop, a chair pushed too close, a cable snagged on the frame, and stopped itself rather than keep pushing. Clear whatever's in the way, then press either arrow again to resume.

Q: What does E02 mean?
A: E02 means the two leg motors have drifted out of sync with each other, so one leg is reading a slightly different height than the other even though they're bolted to the same frame. It shows up most often after a power interruption mid-move. A recalibration, holding the down arrow until the desk fully lowers and the display flashes, resets both legs to the same reference point.

Q: What does E03 mean?
A: E03 points to a communication fault between the control pad and the motor box, usually a loose cable connector at the crossbar rather than anything electrical failing outright. Check that the flat cable running from the pad down the leg to the control box is fully seated at both ends before assuming the worst.

Q: What does E04 mean?
A: E04 is an internal control box fault that doesn't clear with recalibration or with unplugging the desk for a minute and plugging it back in. This is the one code we can't talk you through over email.

## Fixing it yourself vs. contacting support

Q: Which codes can I fix myself?
A: E01 through E03, most of the time. Clear the obstruction, recalibrate, or reseat the cable, and the code should clear within a minute.

Q: When should I just contact support?
A: If E04 shows up, or if E02 or E03 comes right back within a day of clearing it. At that point the fix is usually a replacement control box, LS-ACC-010, and support needs your #LS order number to send the right part for your desk size.
