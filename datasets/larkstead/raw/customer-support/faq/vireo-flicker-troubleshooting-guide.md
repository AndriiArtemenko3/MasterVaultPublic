## Are you seeing this?

Q: My lamp flickers at night, is this a known issue?
A: If you bought your Vireo lamp (LS-LMP-001) in October 2025 or later and it's running firmware v1.2, yes, we know about this. The symptom is a slow, visible flicker, roughly one pulse every two seconds, that shows up once the sleep-mode dimming schedule brings brightness down below about 20 percent. If your lamp only flickers at that low, dim setting and stays steady the rest of the time, this guide is for you.

Q: How do I check my firmware version?
A: Open the Vireo app, tap your lamp, then Settings, About. If it reads v1.2, you're on the version this guide covers.

## The workaround

Q: How do I stop the flicker right now?
A: In the app, go to Settings, then Sleep schedule, and toggle sleep-mode dimming off. The flicker stops immediately because the lamp no longer steps down into the brightness range where it happens. The tradeoff is your lamp stays at its regular brightness overnight instead of dimming automatically, so turn it off by hand if you want it dark.

Q: I already tried a different power adapter, it didn't help. Is that expected?
A: Yes. This isn't a power problem, swapping the power adapter (LS-ACC-012) doesn't change the flicker at all, so there's no need to buy or request a replacement adapter for this specific issue.

## What's next

Q: Is there a real fix coming?
A: We're working with our firmware vendor on a permanent fix. We don't have a release date to share yet, but this page will update once one is confirmed.

Q: How will I know when it's fixed?
A: If you've already contacted support about this, reply to your existing HD ticket and we'll update you directly when the fix ships. If you haven't opened a ticket yet, you don't need to; just use the workaround above in the meantime.

Q: Does this affect every Vireo lamp?
A: No. Only units built on a specific circuit board revision seem to show it, and only on firmware v1.2. If your lamp was purchased before October 2025, you're very unlikely to see this.
