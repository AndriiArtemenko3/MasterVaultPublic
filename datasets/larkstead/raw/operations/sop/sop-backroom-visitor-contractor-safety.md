SOP: Visitor and contractor safety, Portland showroom backroom
Effective: 2026-05-11
Owner: Hank Morrow (HM)
Applies to: Warehouse, showroom staff, any visiting contractor
Systems: sign-in sheet, showroom key log

Purpose
1 dock door, 1 pallet jack, 1 walk-behind forklift, that's the whole
footprint back there but it still moves stock every day. Keep anyone not
signed in and vested off the dock line.

Prerequisites
- Visitor or contractor's name on file with Hank or whoever's covering
- High-vis vests at the door, 6 on hand at any time
- Dock door clear of standing stock before any vehicle backs in

Steps
1. Sign in at the clipboard by the door. Name, company, time in. No
   exceptions for regulars, the ParcelPoint driver signs in same as anyone
   else.
2. Put on a vest before crossing the yellow line 4 ft inside the dock door.
   Anyone past that line without one gets sent back to sign-in, no
   exceptions for a five-minute drop-off.
3. Check with whoever's running the forklift before walking the dock path.
   The forklift has the right of way in the dock zone, always, foot traffic
   waits.
4. A delivery truck backing in: stay behind the yellow line until it's
   stopped and chocked. Trucks back in blind here, there's no spotter mirror
   on that wall, so give it the full stop before crossing.
5. Contractors doing overhead or fixed work, shelving, lighting, HVAC, get a
   second sign-off from Hank before starting, logged separately from the
   general visitor sheet.
6. Sign out on the way through the door. Time out matters as much as time in
   if anything gets checked later.

Escalation
Anyone crossing the line without a vest twice in one visit gets asked to
leave for the day. A near-miss with the forklift, vest or no vest, gets
reported to Hank same day, verbal is fine, written follow-up by end of week.

Close-out
Sign-in sheet gets filed weekly, Friday, whether anything happened or not. A
near-miss report gets its own line in the log, dated, no rounding the time.
