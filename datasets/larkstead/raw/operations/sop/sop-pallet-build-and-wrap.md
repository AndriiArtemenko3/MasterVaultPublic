SOP: Pallet build and wrap
Effective: 2024-08-26
Owner: Hank Morrow (HM)
Applies to: warehouse crew
Systems: ParcelPoint portal

Purpose
Build and wrap outbound pallets so freight reaches the dock undamaged and the carrier can read the label from six feet back, every load, every weight class.

Prerequisites
- Weight class known for every unit going on the pallet, light, medium, or heavy per the SKU table
- Shrink wrap, corner boards, and pallet caps stocked at the build station

Steps
1. Sort by weight class before stacking. Heavy items, meaning desk frames, chair bases, and bundle components, go bottom layer only. Never stack heavy on top of light or medium cartons.
2. Build heavy cartons in a brick pattern, alternating orientation layer to layer, three layers max before the pallet needs a second base.
3. Light and medium cartons stack up to six layers, corners aligned column-style, not bricked, since bricking crushes light cartons in the middle of the stack.
4. Square every layer against the pallet edge within a half inch. A corner overhanging more than that catches on dock doors and racking on the other end.
5. Wrap in a spiral pattern: minimum 3 turns at the base, 2 turns at each corner going up, and a full cap turn at the top. Sixteen total turns is the floor for anything over 4 feet tall.
6. Place the carrier label on the long side facing the dock door, never on top. A label the loader can't see without tipping the pallet gets missed at pickup.
7. Log the pallet count, weight class mix, and carrier pickup slot in the ParcelPoint portal before end of shift. Name the load by carrier and weekday in the note, a wednesday cascadia load, say, same as the shorthand around the dock.
8. Photograph the finished pallet from two angles before it leaves the build station. This is the record if a damage claim comes back on the load later.

Escalation
Any pallet over 1,500 lbs total or taller than 6 feet needs a second person to confirm the wrap before staging. Don't stage one like that solo.

Close-out
Finished pallet count and photos go in the ParcelPoint portal same day. A wrap failure caught at the dock, loose wrap or a leaning stack, gets rebuilt before pickup, not patched with extra tape.
