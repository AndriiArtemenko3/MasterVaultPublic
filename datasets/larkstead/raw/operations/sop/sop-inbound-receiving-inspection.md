SOP: Inbound receiving inspection
Effective: 2024-02-12
Owner: Hank Morrow (HM)
Applies to: warehouse receiving crew
Systems: Ledgerly, ParcelPoint portal

Purpose
Catch shortages and damage on every inbound vendor shipment before it posts as received stock, so Ledgerly never carries a quantity that isn't actually on the shelf.

Prerequisites
- Vendor packing slip or invoice copy at the dock before the truck is unloaded
- Ledgerly receiving screen open at the dock

Steps
1. Match carton count against the vendor invoice or packing slip before anything moves off the pallet. Ostrava and Verdant loads arrive as full containers through Cascadia Freight. Count by carton, not by a weight guess.
2. Open one carton in five for Ostrava frame shipments, one in ten for Verdant mat cartons and Meridian small-parts cartons. Full unwrap isn't required, a corner check and a lift test.
3. Photograph any crushed corner, torn shrink wrap, or water staining before touching the contents further. Four angles minimum: two corners, the carrier label, the pallet as a whole.
4. If the carton count on the dock doesn't match the invoice, hold the full pallet at the dock and don't break down any cartons until Dmitri Okafor has looked at the paperwork and decided whether it's a carrier loss, a vendor shortage, or a clerical error on the slip.
5. Enter the confirmed quantity in Ledgerly against the matching PO line the same day. Note any lot number printed on the vendor slip in the receiving record.
6. Heavy items, meaning Birch frames, Rowan bases, and bundled components, route to the ParcelPoint queue for the Reno transfer. Light and medium cartons shelve directly in the Portland backroom.
7. Any single carton with damage across more than 10% of its contents gets pulled to the damage shelf, tagged, and held for the carton damage claim SOP rather than shelved as good stock.

Escalation
Count variance over 2% of the invoiced quantity, or any pallet still held at end of shift, goes to Dmitri same day. He calls it.

Close-out
A receiving run closes when Ledgerly shows a confirmed quantity matching the physical count and any damage photos are attached to the record. Discrepancies stay open until Dmitri signs off.

References
- Cascadia Freight (VEN-06) runs Ostrava and Verdant inbound containers
- ParcelPoint Fulfillment (VEN-04) contract covers heavy-item warehousing from 2023-11-01
- Carton damage claim SOP, once published, for damaged cartons pulled at step 7
