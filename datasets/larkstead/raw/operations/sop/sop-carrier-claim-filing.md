SOP: Carrier claim filing across carrier types
Effective: 2025-08-25
Owner: Dmitri Okafor (DO)
Applies to: operations, warehouse lead
Systems: Ledgerly, ParcelPoint portal

Purpose
Three claim paths exist depending on where the damage or loss happened, and this SOP puts one evidence standard and one Ledgerly recovery format behind all three so a claim never gets rebuilt from scratch because the file was thin.

Prerequisites
- Damage or loss already confirmed, not suspected
- Invoice or order number for the shipment on hand before filing starts

Steps
1. Sort the claim by path first. Inbound freight damage from an Ostrava Metalworks or Verdant Textiles container goes to Cascadia Freight direct. Outbound heavy freight, desks and bundles moving through the ParcelPoint warehouse, goes through the ParcelPoint portal's claims module. Outbound light and medium parcels still shipping from the Portland backroom go through the backroom's small-parcel carrier account, filed by whoever packed the order.
2. Build the evidence package before touching a claim form: photos from at least two angles, the invoice or order number, the carrier's load or tracking number, and the affected quantity spelled out, 3 units, not "a few."
3. Inbound: file with Cascadia Freight inside their 5 business day window from receipt. Miss it and the claim is dead. No exceptions.
4. ParcelPoint-handled outbound: file inside the portal within 10 calendar days of the customer or the warehouse first reporting the damage.
5. Backroom outbound: file with the small-parcel carrier account within 5 business days of the report, same window as inbound, since it runs through the same carrier relationship on the receiving side.
6. Log the claim number, filing date, carrier, and claimed dollar amount in Ledgerly as a pending recovery. Claimed figure is always landed cost, never list price.
7. If the carrier declines, book the loss as scrap at landed cost. A write-off from 500.00 to 999.99 needs my sign-off; at or above 1000.00, Mara signs off, per the expense policy effective 2025-07-01.
8. Close the Ledgerly entry when the carrier pays, declines, or 30 days pass with no response, whichever comes first.

Escalation
Any single claim over 500.00, or three or more claims against one carrier inside a month, comes to me the same day regardless of where it sits in the steps above.

Close-out
25 Aug: 6 claims open across the three paths, 4 Cascadia, 1 ParcelPoint, 1 backroom. Running count goes in the weekly ops log until each entry closes.
