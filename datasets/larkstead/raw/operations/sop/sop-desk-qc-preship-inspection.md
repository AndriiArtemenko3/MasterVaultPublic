SOP: Desk QC pre-ship inspection
Effective: 2025-03-03
Owner: Hank Morrow (HM)
Applies to: warehouse crew
Systems: Ledgerly

Purpose
Catch a bad motor, a loose frame joint, or a faulting control box before a Birch desk gets cartoned, not after a customer reports it. Every unit gets the same three checks regardless of size.

Prerequisites
- Desk frame and control box assembled and powered, not mid-build
- A flat section of floor with room to run the desk through its full height range

Steps
1. Power the unit and run it from lowest to highest setting, then back down. Listen for grinding, watch for stutter on the display, and time the full run. Anything running over 24 seconds one way on the 48 in and 60 in frames, or over 30 seconds one way on the 72 in frame, which carries a longer travel distance and a heavier load, fails the motor check outright.
2. Check the control box display for an error code at both ends of the run. A clean run shows no code. Any code, log it exactly as displayed, don't paraphrase it.
3. If the motor check or the control box check fails, pull the unit off the line and swap the motor control box before re-running step 1. Do not carton a unit that failed either check on the first attempt.
4. Spot-check frame torque at the four leg-to-frame bolts with the torque driver. Each bolt should hold at the marked setting without back-off. A bolt that spins free or won't seat gets re-torqued once. One shot only.
5. If a re-torqued bolt still won't hold, the frame casting itself is suspect, not the bolt. Pull the whole frame and tag it for the damage shelf rather than shipping it.
6. Once all three checks pass clean, mark the unit ready for cartoning on the floor tag. A unit without a dated pass mark does not get cartoned, no matter how the queue is running.
7. Log the pass in the daily QC count. One line: date, desk size, pass or the specific fail reason.

Escalation
Two failed units of the same frame size in one shift goes to Dmitri same day, since that usually means a bad batch from Ostrava rather than one-off assembly trouble.

Close-out
A shift's QC log closes with total units checked and total units failed, by reason. Failed units stay off the floor until the motor control box or the frame gets swapped and the unit re-passes.
