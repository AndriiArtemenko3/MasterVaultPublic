Project: Desk carton redesign, LS-DSK-001 line
Owner: Hank Morrow (HM), co-owner Dmitri Okafor (DO)
Opened: 2025-02-03   Target close: 2025-04-25
Related: VEN-05

Goal
Cut desk shipping damage claims from 5.6% of shipments (34 of 612 in Q4 2024) to under 3% by relaunching all three Birch desk cartons, LS-DSK-001-48, -60, and -72, with molded pulp corner blocks by 15 Apr 2025.

flat foam corner pads have been the carton standard since the desk line launched in 2022 and they crush. every claim photo from the last two quarters shows the same failure: a crushed corner, a cracked leg bracket underneath it. GreenCrate's (VEN-05) recycled-content stock is fine, the corner is the weak point, not the board.

Plan
- Design: GreenCrate swaps the flat corner pads for molded pulp corner blocks across all three carton sizes. First samples due 21 Feb.
- Drop test: 20 drops per carton size from 30 inches, 6 orientations each. Corner crush past 1/4 inch of compression counts as a failure.
- Field pilot: 40 cartons of the 60 in size, the highest-claim size, ship with new corners starting 10 Mar, tracked against old-corner cartons shipping the same three weeks.
- Full switch: all three sizes move to new corners by 01 Apr, two weeks of buffer ahead of the 15 Apr target to absorb a slow GreenCrate print run.

Risks
- Molded pulp adds thickness and the 72 in carton no longer fits the current pallet stack height: fallback is dropping that size's pallet count from 24 cartons to 20, warehouse floor plan otherwise unchanged.
- Print run slips past March: fallback is Hank holds a 200-carton buffer of the old design for April orders while GreenCrate catches up.
