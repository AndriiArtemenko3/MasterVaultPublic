Date: 2024-12-03
Filed by: Hank Morrow (HM)
Filed with: Ostrava Metalworks (VEN-02) -- copy only, no corrective action requested
System under report: LS-STD-001 Wren laptop stand, hinge assembly, LOT-2024-40
Related tickets: none -- proactive sample check, no customer reports tied to this batch

Summary
Hinge hold-angle on the November Ostrava batch runs wider than the last few batches across a 40-unit sample. Still inside spec. Worth watching on the next receipt.

Sample check steps
1. Pulled 40 units at random from LOT-2024-40, received 22 Nov, out of roughly 620 units in the lot.
2. Clamped each stand base, loaded the platform arm with a 2 lb test weight, and recorded the hinge hold angle off a 90 degree start.
3. Readings ran 84 to 96 degrees against a spec band of 80 to 100. Every unit passed.

Affected population
40 of roughly 620 units in LOT-2024-40 sampled. Zero failures, so there is no return or rework population behind this note, just a wider spread than usual worth tracking.

Evidence
Cross-checked against LOT-2024-25, the September receipt, which held a tighter 89 to 91 degree spread on its own 40-unit sample from the same hinge supplier line. No customer tickets tie back to LOT-2024-40 yet, and the wider spread hasn't produced a single field failure so far, which is the main reason this stays a monitoring note instead of a hold.

Ask
Log LOT-2024-40 as a watch flag. Pull another 40-unit sample off the next Ostrava Wren shipment and compare the spread before closing the flag. No vendor corrective action requested at this time, this is a heads-up copy for Ostrava's QA contact, not an open issue.
