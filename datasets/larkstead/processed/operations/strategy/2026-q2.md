## Theme

Hold the ParcelPoint cutover steady through its first full quarter live, and keep vendor quality gates tight after two Verdant flags in six months. The cutover is done, not still being decided, so this quarter is about proving it out rather than debating it further.

## Ranked goals

### 1. Clear the ParcelPoint acknowledgment-rate revert threshold every week
Success criteria: order-acknowledgment rate stays above the 95% revert trigger for the full quarter, with zero invocations of the standby rollback plan.
Key dependency: the idempotency-key fix on the Shopstack webhook needs to hold up under real, non-sandbox traffic volume, not just the throttled test runs that originally found the duplicate-order bug.
Blocking risk: Q1 was the first full quarter live; Q2 is the first quarter where a slow seasonal dip or spike tests the threshold under conditions the original cutover checklist didn't specifically rehearse.

### 2. Prove the Verdant QC addendum holds on live lots
Success criteria: every Verdant lot received in Q2 clears the AQL 2.5 pre-shipment sample and the humidity-cycle test with no lot crossing the 2% automatic-credit trigger.
Key dependency: Dmitri's weekly defect-count reporting cadence needs to actually run every week, not just exist as a written commitment.
Blocking risk: the November 2025 armrest-pad watch flag was on a different SKU line than the original mat defect, meaning the addendum's mat-specific terms may not fully cover the risk surface that flag exposed.

### 3. Run the full hardware-release matrix on every Vireo firmware push
Success criteria: zero staged rollouts proceed without a clean bench pass on both rev C and rev D units, per the SOP written after the v1.2 flicker incident.
Key dependency: at least 3 bench units on each hardware revision need to be available and current before any release candidate ships to Bitgrove for staging.
Blocking risk: rev D was only qualified in March 2026, so the test matrix is still young; a gap in bench-unit availability could force a choice between delaying a release and skipping a check the SOP requires.

## What this rules out

No re-opening the switch-mat-vendor decision this quarter absent a confirmed second defect; the addendum gets one full quarter to prove itself before that conversation restarts. No second 3PL or fulfillment change while the ParcelPoint cutover is still in its first full quarter of live monitoring.

## Time budget

Roughly 35% of operations engineering time on ParcelPoint monitoring and the acknowledgment-rate dashboard, 35% on vendor QC tracking across Verdant and Meridian, 20% on the Vireo hardware-matrix cadence, 10% in reserve for whichever of the three surfaces something first.
