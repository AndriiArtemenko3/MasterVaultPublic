## Question

Should Larkstead drop Verdant Textiles Co. (VEN-01) as the Alder mat supplier after LOT-2025-14 shipped with an edge-stitch delamination defect?

## Options

**Option A: Terminate the Verdant contract and re-source Alder mats from a second supplier.**
Removes the immediate defect risk but forces a requalification cycle on tooling, color match, and lead time with no guarantee the new vendor performs better.

**Option B: Keep Verdant, absorb the claim, and add no new controls.**
Cheapest short-term path. Leaves Larkstead exposed to a repeat of the same failure mode with the next lot.

**Option C: Keep Verdant, file a formal defect claim, and bind them to new pre-shipment QC gates.**
Preserves an established 2023 supply relationship and known unit economics while adding inspection controls Verdant did not previously carry.

## Evidence

- QA cull confirmed a defect pattern in LOT-2025-14: an 8.5% rate on a 200-unit sample, consistent with the 9.07% full-lot figure (bug-report-bug-report-lot-2025-14-edge-stitch-delamination-05).
- A formal claim against Verdant, tied to INV-VRD-2025-118, was already planned before the sample even cleared (bug-report-bug-report-lot-2025-14-edge-stitch-delamination-14).
- Mara Voss's signed claim demanded 1,841.50 in credit: 1,638.30 for the 127 defective units plus 203.20 in pro-rated freight (memo-letter-larkstead-verdant-defect-claim-09).
- Verdant accepted the goods credit but declined the freight portion, citing contract terms (memo-letter-verdant-response-credit-offer-03).
- Verdant separately offered third-party pre-shipment inspection, at its own cost, on the next three lots (memo-letter-verdant-response-credit-offer-06).
- The incident's cumulative cost reached 2,056.35 before the CEO approval threshold was triggered (memo-memo-refund-exception-lot-2025-14-03).
- A second, unrelated Verdant quality flag hit the Rowan armrest pad line in November 2025: stitch-tension readings ran low enough to set a watch flag on the whole VEN-01 relationship for that SKU (bug-report-bug-report-verdant-armrest-pad-stitch-te-3416f1fe-11).
- A prior labeling compliance gap on Verdant material declarations was caught and corrected the same quarter, a smaller but related discipline signal (compliance-note-compliance-note-verdant-textile-disclosure-03).

## Criteria

Landed cost per unit, lead time to requalify a new supplier, Verdant's willingness to accept binding QC terms, and how much of the 2,056.35 incident cost is recoverable through credit.

## Recommendation

Option C. Verdant moved fast on the credit and volunteered inspection costs it did not have to offer, which is a stronger signal than a first-lot defect alone would justify termination for. A four-year relationship with known tooling and pricing is worth one structured chance under tighter gates rather than a blind re-sourcing cycle.

## What would change my mind

A confirmed defect on any lot after the QC addendum takes effect, or a second Verdant lot crossing the 2% automatic-credit threshold within the same fiscal year. The November armrest pad watch flag is the leading indicator to track first.

## Next action

Dmitri Okafor tracks the addendum's humidity-cycle pass rate on every incoming Verdant lot and reports defect counts weekly. Revisit this decision if the armrest pad watch flag escalates to a confirmed defect.
