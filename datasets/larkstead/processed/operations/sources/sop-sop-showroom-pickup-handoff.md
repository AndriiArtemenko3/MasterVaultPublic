## Content

SOP: Showroom pickup handoff
Effective: 2024-06-17
Owner: Priya Raman (PR)
Applies to: showroom front-desk staff, Portland
Systems: Helprise, Ledgerly

Purpose
Get customers their order quickly and correctly at the Portland showroom, and keep unclaimed stock from sitting on the pickup shelf indefinitely, which just ties up space and worries the customer when they finally do call.

Prerequisites
- Order marked ready for pickup in Helprise, with the order number confirmed
- Pickup shelf has space; last week's holds cleared per step 6

Steps
1. Confirm the order number the customer gives against Helprise. If they don't have it, look up by name and the last four digits of the phone number on file, and confirm out loud before pulling anything.
2. Ask for photo ID before handing anything over. Match the name on the ID against the name on the order, not just a rough resemblance.
3. Stage every ready order on the pickup shelf the morning it's marked ready, labeled with the order number and the date it landed on the shelf.
4. Hand off the full order in one trip. If a component shipped separately, a mat or a monitor arm, tell the customer before they leave, not after.
5. Mark the order picked up in Helprise the moment it leaves the building, with the date and staff initials.
6. An order untouched after 7 calendar days gets a courtesy call or email. Still untouched 7 days after that contact, or 14 days total with no response, cancel it, return the stock to inventory, and refund the original payment method.
7. Log the return-to-stock in Ledgerly the same day, referencing the original order number.

Example: an order staged 03 Jun and never claimed gets the courtesy contact on 10 Jun, and returns to stock 17 Jun if there's still no answer.

Escalation
A customer disputing an ID mismatch, or asking for a hold past 14 days, goes to Priya. Don't extend a hold on your own judgment, folks tend to ask nicely and it's still not your call.

Close-out
Each week's shelf gets checked against Helprise for anything crossing the 7-day or 14-day mark, so nothing quietly ages past either window unnoticed.
