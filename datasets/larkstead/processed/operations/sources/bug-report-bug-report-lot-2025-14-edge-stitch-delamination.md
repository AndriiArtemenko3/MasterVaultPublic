## Content

Bug report: lot-2025-14-edge-stitch-delamination
Date: 2025-08-04
Filed by: Dmitri Okafor (DO)
Filed with: Verdant Textiles Co. (VEN-01)
System under report: Alder desk mat, LOT-2025-14 (all three colorways)
Related tickets: 8 to date, first HD-2025-04217 (05 Jul), latest HD-2025-04415 (03 Aug)

Summary
1400 units of LOT-2025-14 came in 19 Jun. 214 shipped to customers between
20 Jun and 31 Jul before the pattern was flagged. 1186 units remained on
hand when I pulled a sample yesterday, and a subset of that lot is
delaminating at the edge.

Reproduction steps
1. Take a LOT-2025-14 unit, any of the three colors.
2. Flex the edge binding by hand or leave the mat under normal desk use
   for two to three weeks.
3. In affected units, the edge binding separates from the mat core and
   the edge lifts, sometimes over half an inch.
4. Put a suspect unit in the humidity chamber at 85% RH for 48 hours: the
   lift accelerates and becomes visible within that window even on units
   that looked borderline at pull.

Affected population
1400 units total, 600 LS-MAT-001-CHL, 500 LS-MAT-001-SND, 300
LS-MAT-001-SGE. 214 units already shipped to customers, 20 Jun through 31
Jul. 1186 units remained on hand at the warehouse when I inspected 04 Aug.

I ran a sample inspection of 200 units drawn evenly across all three
colors and found 17 defective, 8.5% of the sample. That is high enough
that I pulled the full remaining lot rather than trust the sample alone.
Full cull of all 1186 units is done as of this afternoon: 109 units set
aside for scrap, edge-stitch gap or lift beyond what Hank and I agreed is
the cutoff. The other 1077 cleared units are in quarantine, not released,
pending a second humidity-cycle test on 8 sample units at 85% RH for 48
hours, results due 11 Aug. I don't want to release stock off one sample
pull if the cull rate on a bigger check comes back different.

Evidence
8 confirmed tickets to date, all on orders placed 21 Jun or later, which
tracks with LOT-2025-14 hitting shelves right around then. Every failure
so far is the same mechanism, edge binding separating from the core, not
a tear, not a stain, not a manufacturing gap in the mat surface itself.
Nothing on pre-19 Jun stock reproduces this in the chamber, which is
another reason I'm treating this as a lot issue and not a design issue
across the whole Alder line.

Ask
Three things, and I'd like movement on all three this week. One, support
needs a standard macro so Priya's team isn't answering these one at a
time anymore, that's costing hours we don't need to spend. Two, we need a
written recall-handling SOP, this won't be the last time we cull a lot
and I don't want to improvise the quarantine process again. Three, once
the 11 Aug humidity result is in, I want a formal defect claim drafted
against Verdant Textiles (VEN-01) tied to INV-VRD-2025-118. We're at 109
culled plus 8 field failures so far, 117 units and climbing while tickets
are still coming in, so I'd rather wait for the 11 Aug number before we
put a final count in front of Mara.
