## Content

Bug report: verdant-rowan-armrest-pad-stitch-tension-watch
Date: 2025-11-18
Filed by: Dmitri Okafor (DO)
Filed with: Verdant Textiles Co. (VEN-01)
System under report: Rowan armrest pad, pair (replacement), LS-ACC-009, LOT-2025-61
Related tickets: 0 to date, caught at receiving before any units cleared to stock

Summary
480 units of LS-ACC-009 came in on LOT-2025-61, 04 Nov. A 60-unit pull-test
sample cleared the stitch-tension floor but skewed low enough that I'm
setting a watch flag on the lot rather than releasing it clean.

Reproduction steps
1. Draw 60 units at random from LOT-2025-61 (480 units total, received 04
   Nov).
2. Run the standard outer-seam pull-test. Spec is 17.5 to 25.0 lbf.
3. Log each reading rather than a straight pass or fail. That's the only way
   a cluster near the floor shows up instead of getting buried inside an
   otherwise-passing sample.

Affected population
LOT-2025-61, 480 units of LS-ACC-009, Verdant Textiles (VEN-01), received 04
Nov. Sample pulled 17 Nov, 60 of the 480 units, 12.5 percent of the lot. 11
of the 60 sampled units read between 17.6 and 18.4 lbf, tight against the
17.5 floor. None below it. Readings across the full sample ran 17.6 to 24.8
lbf. The other 420 units in the lot have not been individually pulled.

Evidence
Every one of the 60 passed. No lot rejection, no shipment hold. What
concerns me is the shape of the distribution, not any single reading: 11
units clustered inside a one-lbf band right at the wall we set the floor at,
and I don't have a clean baseline yet on normal lot-to-lot spread for this
SKU to say whether that's drift or just how this batch came off the line.
Two prior LS-ACC-009 lots this year didn't get individual readings logged,
only pass or fail, so I can't compare against them directly. That gap is on
us, not Verdant.

Ask
Watch flag on VEN-01 for the LS-ACC-009 line, effective now. If the next lot,
expected early Feb, shows the same low cluster on its sample, I want to
escalate to a written request for their stitch-tension process check before
we take a third lot on trust. In the meantime, have Hank's team log
individual lbf readings on every pull-test going forward, not just
pass/fail, so the next report has something real to compare against.
