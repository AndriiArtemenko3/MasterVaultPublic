## Content

Competitor: ErgoNest
Card revised: 2025-12-10 by TA
Prior revision: 2025-04-09
Sources: Kestrel Financial Planning showroom walk-in, Grace Liu, 2025-12-04 (PW-kestrel-10seat); ergonest site checked 2025-12-08, the lease program is now listed on their homepage.

## Positioning

Direct-import reseller of white-label ergonomic desks and chairs, still selling on price and shipping speed. New this cycle: a per-seat leasing option, launched sometime late this quarter, aimed squarely at the B2B accounts we're chasing.

## Price posture

Still the 10-18% band on outright purchase. The lease is a new pricing shape, not a discount on the old one, and we don't have a clean per-seat monthly figure logged yet. Grace didn't share an exact number and their site doesn't publish one.

## Where they win

- removes the capital ask for a prospect who's nervous about one big number up front, real for a stage like discovery
- monthly framing reads smaller than a lump sum even when the multi-year total isn't

## Where we win

- ownership, full stop. Buy from us once and it's yours: no term to renegotiate, no end-of-lease conversation waiting at year two or three
- the end-of-lease gap is the angle worth pressing. Grace didn't know whether her company would own anything once the term ran out, or would owe a buyout, or would have to ship the furniture back, and neither their rep nor their site gave her a straight answer when she asked
- the parts shelf argument still applies underneath all of this. A leased chair that breaks is still their problem to escalate, not ours

## Ground rules

Ask every leasing-curious prospect the buyout question directly, on the record if we can. We don't have a confirmed answer ourselves yet. Nothing customer-facing names them.
