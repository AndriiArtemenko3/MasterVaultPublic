# Qbr Accessory Attach Rate 2025 05

## Summary

Analysis: accessory attach rate on desk orders Date: 2025-05-06 Author: Sofia Grieg Data: Shopstack export pulled 2025-05-01; standalone desk orders only, trailing two windows of roughly 90 days each. Bundle orders are excluded since Fledgling and Canopy already carry a Sparrow kit as a built-in component, not an attach.

## Content

Analysis: accessory attach rate on desk orders
Date: 2025-05-06
Author: Sofia Grieg
Data: Shopstack export pulled 2025-05-01; standalone desk orders only,
trailing two windows of roughly 90 days each. Bundle orders are excluded
since Fledgling and Canopy already carry a Sparrow kit as a built-in
component, not an attach.

## Question

Is the cable guide actually moving Magpie tray attach, or was Sparrow always going to win this?

## Table

| Accessory | Window 1 (before guide) | Window 2 (after guide) |
|---|---|---|
| Sparrow cable kit | 179 of 298 | 187 of 312 |
| Magpie cable tray | 24 of 298 | 58 of 312 |

## Findings

1. Sparrow attaches to roughly 6 in 10 standalone desk orders in both
   windows, 179 of 298 before and 187 of 312 after. Flat. It was already the
   default add before the guide went out; nothing here moved the needle
   either direction.
2. Magpie moved. 24 of 298 before the guide published on 2025-02-18, 58 of
   312 after, better than double.
3. The two aren't independent. A customer who adds Magpie is very likely
   already adding Sparrow: 46 of the 58 post-guide Magpie buyers also have a
   Sparrow kit in the same order. This reads as a bigger cable-management
   cart overall, not a swap from one accessory to the other.
4. Not every small accessory moved. Aspen felt sleeves sat at 11 of 298
   before and 13 of 312 after, basically flat, and nobody's writing a guide
   about those any time soon. Magpie's jump isn't just "cable content
   published, all cable products rise."
5. At list price, the post-guide window alone puts an extra 1,485.00 on the
   books from Magpie trays that wouldn't have sold under the old rate, 33
   incremental units at 45.00 each, holding the pre-guide 8.05% rate flat
   against the 312-order base. Small, but it's the first accessory we've
   moved with content instead of a price change.

## Caveats

Correlation, not proof. The guide went out the same window as a general
spring uptick in desk orders, so some of the Magpie lift could just be more
total orders, not more attach per order. Bundle orders are out of both counts
on purpose, and B2B seat orders are excluded too since those follow a
different accessory pattern and would muddy a DTC read. The 33-unit
incremental figure assumes the pre-guide rate would have held flat, which is
an assumption, not a measurement.

Next: hold Magpie's listing position through 2025-08 and recheck with a full
two quarters of post-guide data. Owner: SG.
