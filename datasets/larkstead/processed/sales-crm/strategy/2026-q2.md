## Theme

Defend the 35% B2B revenue share against ErgoNest's leasing pitch and a still-fresh discount-tier change, without adding sales headcount. Tom and Yuki carry the full pipeline this year, so the quarter's growth has to come from protecting what is already in motion rather than expanding the team chasing new logos.

## Ranked goals

### 1. Retain both named churn-risk accounts
Success criteria: Stonebridge and Foxglove both renew or explicitly commit to renewal terms before their respective decision points.
Key dependency: the leasing-pilot decision needs to move fast enough to give Foxglove a real answer before its August renewal deadline, not after.
Blocking risk: Foxglove is actively comparing an ErgoNest lease quote against renewal, and Stonebridge is still cooling from last year's mat-defect replacement cycle; either account can be lost for a different reason than the one the team is watching.

### 2. Land the leasing-pilot decision, not just discuss it
Success criteria: a scoped answer, pilot or no pilot, on the open leasing decision before the Q2 review date.
Key dependency: pick the pilot's first account deliberately rather than defaulting to whichever deal is loudest that week; Foxglove's deadline makes it the natural first case if the pilot goes ahead.
Blocking risk: ErgoNest's own end-of-lease terms are unclear even to their sales team, which is an opening Larkstead can only use if its own pilot terms are actually better defined, not just cheaper.

### 3. Confirm the discontinue-a-sku call with one more data pull
Success criteria: a second attach-rate read on Aspen felt sleeves before the August review date, closing that decision either way.
Key dependency: the same QBR process used in May needs to run again on the same measurement basis, so the two reads are comparable.
Blocking risk: if the second read gets skipped or delayed, the SKU stays in limbo past its review date with no forcing function to close it.

## What this rules out

No third sales seat added this quarter; the 2026 planning priority holds until Tom or Yuki is demonstrably over capacity, and Q2 is not expected to cross that line. No blanket price-tier change beyond the one that already shipped in December; that change needs a full quarter to show its effect on realized discount before touching it again.

## Time budget

Roughly 40% of non-quota-carrying time on the two churn-risk accounts, 30% on scoping and running the leasing pilot if it proceeds, 20% on the SKU re-measurement and general catalog hygiene, 10% in reserve.
