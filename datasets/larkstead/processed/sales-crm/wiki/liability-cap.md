## Definition
**Operating:** The current liability cap in sales-CRM practices limits the liability for bodily injury to the value of the order. A liability cap is a contractual provision that sets a maximum limit on the liability that one party may have to another in case of a claim related to the contract.

## Main Insights
- Marcus advocates for the removal of the liability cap entirely for cases involving bodily injury.
- Current terms stipulate that liability for bodily injury is capped at the order's value.
- There is an objection indicating that the present liability cap does not align with the expectations of their malpractice carrier.
- The next action includes sending the liability cap language of Beacon to Colefield & Tran for revision by February 17, 2026.

## Why It Compounds
Understanding and negotiating liability caps is critical because they directly influence the risk allocation between parties in contracts. If a liability cap is perceived as insufficient, it may deter potential partners from engaging in business due to fears of unexpected liabilities. Furthermore, the discussions surrounding the removal or modification of these caps highlight the expectations and perceptions regarding risk in the sales-CRM domain. This can impact how businesses structure their contracts and manage their insurance needs, as well as influence the overall market dynamics based on how liability risks are shared.
