## Definition
**Operating:** In the sales-CRM domain, payment terms typically specify the timeframe in which payment is expected after certain milestones, with common practices including a net 30 or net 45 schedule from the date of shipment or delivery. These terms outline the conditions agreed upon between buyers and vendors for settling invoices.

## Main Insights
- Marcus's firm operates with a standard payment term of net 45 days for vendors, suggesting a more extended cash flow allowance compared to typical agreements.
- Invoices for products are due net 30 days following the shipment date, indicating a clear timeline for financial planning and transaction fulfillment. 
- Payment is consistently due net 30 days from the date of delivery, which reinforces clarity in expectations for all parties involved.

## Why It Compounds
Understanding payment terms is crucial in managing cash flow and maintaining healthy vendor relationships. Clear and agreed-upon payment timelines help businesses synchronize their financial operations, ensuring that suppliers are compensated promptly while enabling buyers to optimize their capital management strategies. Variations in payment terms can significantly impact liquidity and supplier willingness to engage in future transactions, making this concept vital in the sales-CRM framework.
