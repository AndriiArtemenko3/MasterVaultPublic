## Definition
**Operating:** In the sales-CRM domain, a discount policy outlines the specific conditions under which sales representatives can apply discounts on product pricing to encourage volume purchases. This policy establishes guidelines on volume tiers and maximum discount levels that can be offered to customers without requiring managerial approval.

## Main Insights
- A volume discount of 15% is applicable for orders of 25 or more units, as indicated in the pricing sheet from June 2024.
- Sales representatives are generally restricted from offering discounts beyond a 15% tier on their own authority.
- Customer orders with 15 units are considered below the discount threshold, which starts at 25 units.
- There is a unique one-time discount exception of 3% specifically for orders of 30 or more seats, effective from October 6, 2025.

## Why It Compounds
The significance of a discount policy lies in its impact on sales strategy and customer relationships. Well-structured discount policies not only help maintain profitability by setting clear boundaries for discounting but also incentivize larger orders, fostering customer loyalty. By providing structured discounts, companies can better forecast sales, manage inventory, and ultimately drive growth while ensuring that sales representatives have the freedom to negotiate within established limits.
