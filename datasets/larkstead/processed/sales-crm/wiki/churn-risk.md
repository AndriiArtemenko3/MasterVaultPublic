## Definition
**Operating:** Churn risk refers to the potential for existing customers to discontinue their business relationships with a company, evaluated primarily through customer engagement metrics and transactional histories. Organizations in the sales-CRM domain use churn-risk assessments to identify customers likely to leave, allowing for targeted retention strategies.

## Main Insights
- The churn-risk board indicates that Stonebridge's likelihood of discontinuing services is increasing, primarily attributed to dissatisfaction following last summer's shipment of mat-defect replacements.
- Foxglove is currently evaluating a quote for ErgoNest, casting uncertainty over its renewal decision due in August, which could lead to a churn risk if not addressed positively.

## Why It Compounds
Churn risk is a critical indicator for businesses because even minor shifts in customer satisfaction can cascade into significant revenue losses. Organizations rely on retaining existing customers more than acquiring new ones due to the lower costs involved. When several customers begin to exhibit signs of churn risk, it can signal deeper issues within products or services, prompting broader organizational evaluations and possible strategic pivots. Ignoring these signals might lead to higher long-term instability and loss of market credibility.
