## Question

Should Larkstead discontinue the Aspen felt sleeve (LS-ACC-004) given its attach rate has not moved despite a marketing push?

## Options

**Option A: Keep Aspen in the catalog unchanged.**
No disruption to the small number of customers who do buy it, but the SKU keeps consuming catalog and support-documentation space for negligible volume.

**Option B: Discontinue Aspen and fold cable management into the Magpie tray as a single recommended accessory.**
Simplifies the accessory story to one product, but removes an option some customers specifically ask for.

**Option C: Re-price or re-bundle Aspen instead of discontinuing it, and re-measure attach rate next quarter.**
Lower-risk than outright discontinuation, but only worth trying if the flat attach rate is a pricing problem rather than a demand problem.

## Evidence

- The same May 2025 QBR that flagged Aspen also measured Sparrow, which attaches to roughly 6 in 10 standalone desk orders in both windows measured (segment-qbr-analysis-qbr-accessory-attach-rate-2025-05-01), and Magpie, which nearly tripled its attach share after a cable-management guide published 2025-02-18: from 24 of 298 orders to 58 of 312 (segment-qbr-analysis-qbr-accessory-attach-rate-2025-05-02).
- Aspen felt sleeves sat at 11 of 298 orders before the guide and 13 of 312 after (segment-qbr-analysis-qbr-accessory-attach-rate-2025-05-04), roughly 3.7% before and 4.2% after: functionally flat, unlike Magpie's jump on the same guide.
- The guide drove real incremental revenue for Magpie specifically: an extra 1,485.00 on the books in the post-guide window from Magpie trays alone (segment-qbr-analysis-qbr-accessory-attach-rate-2025-05-05).
- The product itself is a simple item: Aspen felt sleeves (LS-ACC-004) bundle 3 to 6 loose cables into one soft tube, with a 3-pack covering most single-monitor setups (faq-help-faq-cable-management-07, faq-help-faq-cable-management-08). The customer-facing FAQ recommends Magpie and Aspen together for full-week cable tidiness (faq-help-faq-cable-management-09), meaning Aspen is currently pitched as a companion product to the one that is actually working.

## Criteria

Attach-rate movement under equal marketing exposure, incremental revenue per SKU, and support/documentation overhead per catalog line, weighed against the small existing customer base already relying on it.

## Recommendation

Option B leans toward the honest read of the evidence: the same guide that tripled Magpie's attach rate left Aspen essentially unchanged, which points to a demand ceiling rather than an awareness problem. But this decision stays open pending one more attach-rate read, since a single guide cycle is a thin sample to end a SKU on.

## What would change my mind

A second measurement window showing Aspen attach rate moving meaningfully off its roughly 4% baseline under any future marketing push, which would suggest the May 2025 flat read was an awareness gap rather than a demand ceiling.

## Next action

Re-pull the accessory attach-rate QBR at the next quarterly cycle before finalizing; if Aspen is still flat, proceed to discontinuation rather than a second re-measurement.
