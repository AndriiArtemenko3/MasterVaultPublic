## Question

Should Larkstead cut the B2B volume discount rate and raise the unit floor to qualify for it?

## Options

**Option A: Keep the 15%-at-25-units tier unchanged.**
No sales friction from a rate cut, but does not address discount spend running above plan.

**Option B: Cut the rate to 12% and raise the floor to 30 units, with signed deals grandfathered.**
Reduces realized discount going forward without touching revenue already under contract, at the cost of some pipeline friction on deals quoted under the old terms.

**Option C: Cut the rate but keep the 25-unit floor.**
Smaller structural change, but does less to address the underlying discount-spend problem since more deals still qualify.

## Evidence

- The original tier: 15% off list for orders of 25 or more units, running since 2024-06-01 (policy-sl3-onepager-b2b-pricing-01).
- Reps could not discount past that 15% ceiling on their own authority; anything deeper required expense-policy approval (policy-sl3-onepager-b2b-pricing-05, policy-sl3-onepager-b2b-pricing-06).
- The stated reason for the change: realized B2B discount ran above plan for two straight quarters in 2025 (policy-sl3-policy-b2b-tiers-v2-03).
- The new tier: 12% off list for orders of 30 or more units, effective 2025-12-08, explicitly replacing the 15%-at-25+ tier (policy-sl3-policy-b2b-tiers-v2-01, policy-sl3-policy-b2b-tiers-v2-02).
- Deals signed before the change keep their original contract pricing regardless of the new tier (policy-sl3-policy-b2b-tiers-v2-06); one closed-won order for Cobalt Dental Group is on record at the old 15% rate (policy-sl3-policy-b2b-tiers-v2-07).
- A one-time 3% exception exists for 30-plus-seat deals, requiring CEO sign-off at a 1,000.00 threshold (policy-sl3-policy-b2b-tiers-v2-08).
- The change produced live pipeline friction: an account quoted the old 15% tier in January stayed cool on a later quote under the new terms (email-thread-email-tom-mara-q2-pipeline-2026-06-04).

## Criteria

Reduction in realized discount spend against plan, size of the pipeline segment that falls between the old 25-unit floor and the new 30-unit floor, and whether the exception path is generous enough to save large deals that would otherwise stall.

## Recommendation

Option B, which is what shipped. Grandfathering signed deals avoids re-opening closed contracts, and the exception path gives sales a controlled way to save large deals without reopening the tier itself to every account.

## What would change my mind

If deals in the 25-to-29-unit band that previously qualified start stalling in negotiation at a rate visibly higher than before the change, the floor increase would be creating more lost pipeline than the rate cut is saving in discount spend.

## Next action

Sales must pull the old 2024-06 pricing one-pager off the shared drive so reps stop quoting stale terms (policy-sl3-policy-b2b-tiers-v2-09); track whether the Nordlicht-style stall pattern recurs on other accounts quoted before 2025-12-08.
