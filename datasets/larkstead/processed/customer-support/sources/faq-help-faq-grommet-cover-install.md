## Fit and sizing

Q: What hole size does the Willow grommet cover fit?
A: It's built for round desktop cutouts between 2 and 2.5 inches across. Most Birch desk hardware kits use a 2 inch hole, so a stock Birch desktop is a straightforward fit. If you drilled your own hole in a different desk, measure it before ordering.

Q: Does it work with a square or oval cutout?
A: No. The cover is a round cap over a round sleeve, and it won't seat properly in a square or oval opening. Custom desks with non-round cutouts aren't a match for this part.

Q: Does one pair cover a two-hole desk?
A: Yes. LS-ACC-005 ships as a pair, one cover for each side of a standard dual-monitor-arm cutout layout. Order two pairs if your desk has four holes.

## Installing it

Q: How does it attach?
A: The sleeve drops into the hole from above and the cap threads onto it from below, so you'll need access under the desk for the first install. Hand-tighten only. A quarter turn past snug is plenty.

Q: Any tips for keeping the desktop from getting scratched during install?
A: Set a folded towel or the original packing foam under the cover before you start threading it, since the cap can slip in your fingers and drag across the finish if the desk is bare, and on the birch veneer desktops especially that drag leaves a mark that doesn't buff out. Lay the desk flat if you can rather than working on it upright.

## Removing it

Q: How do I take it out without scratching the desk?
A: Loosen the cap from underneath first, fully, before you touch the top sleeve. Trying to pry the top piece out while the cap is still threaded on is the single biggest cause of scratched veneer around the hole. Once the cap is off, the sleeve lifts straight up with light finger pressure. No tools needed.

Q: I can't get a grip on the cap. What now?
A: A pair of rubber-jawed pliers works better than bare hands and won't mark the metal. Skip anything metal-jawed near the desktop edge.
