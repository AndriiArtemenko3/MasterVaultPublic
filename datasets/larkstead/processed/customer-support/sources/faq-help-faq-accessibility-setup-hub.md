## Assembling with one hand or limited grip strength

Q: Can I put a Birch desk together with one hand?
A: Mostly, yes. The crossbar uses 4 captive bolts that thread by hand for the first several turns before you need a wrench for the final quarter turn, so you can seat all four loosely, check the frame is square, and only then go back for the tightening pass one bolt at a time. The leg-to-frame brackets are the same, hand-start then one final turn each.

Q: What about the Rowan chair?
A: The base and casters press in without tools. The seat-to-mechanism bolts are the one step that really wants two hands or a helper, four bolts, snug them in any order, then go around once more for final torque.

Q: Is there a version of the instructions with fewer steps per page?
A: Ask support for the large-step insert before your order ships and we'll include a version with one action per page instead of three, easier to hold in place with a weight or clip while you work.

## Lever and control reach

Q: The desk's control paddle sits on the right and that doesn't work for me. Can it move?
A: Yes. The paddle bracket has mounting points on both sides of the frame's front rail, so tell us left or right when you place the order and it ships already mounted where you need it. Moving it yourself after the fact takes about 10 minutes and one hex key.

Q: Same question for the chair's recline lever.
A: The standard Rowan ships with the lever under the right front edge of the seat. If reach is an issue, note it on your order and we'll build yours with the lever relocated to the left before it leaves the warehouse; this can't be swapped after assembly without returning the mechanism.

## Booking extra time when your order arrives

Q: Can I get more time at the door when the delivery truck shows up?
A: Yes. The standard freight appointment window is 2 hours. If you need more time to manage boxes, clear a path, or have someone assist you, contact support at least 2 business days before your ship date with your order number and we'll flag the account for a 4 hour window and a threshold delivery instead of curbside drop-off.

Q: What if I can't give 2 days' notice?
A: Call anyway. The carrier can sometimes accommodate a same-day extension, it just isn't guaranteed the way an advance flag is.
