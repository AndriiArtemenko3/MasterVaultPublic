## Finding a sit-stand rhythm

Q: How often should I switch between sitting and standing?
A: Most people start with something like 45 minutes sitting followed by 15 minutes standing, then adjust from there once the desk stops feeling like a novelty. If your legs or lower back complain in the first standing block, shorten it to 10 minutes and build up over two or three weeks rather than forcing a full hour on day one.

Q: Do I need to follow a strict timer?
A: No. Plenty of customers just stand up when a call starts or when they hit a mental wall, and that works fine.

Q: Is there such a thing as standing too much?
A: Yes, especially early on. Eight hours of standing with no sitting breaks strains the same joints a chair strains in reverse, so aim for a mix across the day instead of treating standing as the healthy choice and sitting as the one to avoid.

## Reducing fatigue while standing

Q: Do I need an anti-fatigue mat?
A: Not required, but most people who stand for more than two or three hours a day notice their feet and lower back tire faster on a bare hard floor than on a mat with some give to it. Carpeted offices see less benefit from a mat than a showroom's polished concrete or a home office over tile.

Q: What about footwear?
A: Skip anything with a rigid, unpadded sole for long standing stretches. Bare feet or soft-soled shoes both work better than dress shoes.

Q: Should I shift my weight around while standing?
A: Yes. Staying locked in one position for the whole 15 or 20 minutes defeats a lot of the point, so shift foot to foot, bend a knee now and then, and take a lap around the room if a call allows it.

## Using the memory height presets

Q: How do I save a height to the memory buttons?
A: Move the desk to the height you want, then press and hold one of the numbered buttons, 1, 2, or 3, for about 2 seconds until the display flashes to confirm the save. Tap that same number later and the desk drives itself back to that saved height without you touching the up or down arrows.

Q: What heights should I program in?
A: Set one preset at your seated elbow height and one at your standing elbow height, with your shoulders relaxed and your forearms roughly level with the desktop in both positions. A third preset is handy for a perching height in between if you split time between a stool and full standing.

Q: Can I overwrite a saved preset?
A: Yes, any time. The desk only remembers the most recent height stored under each number.
