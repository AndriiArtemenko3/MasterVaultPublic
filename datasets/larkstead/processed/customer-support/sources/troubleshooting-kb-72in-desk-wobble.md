# Kb 72In Desk Wobble

## Symptom

The customer says the desk rocks side to side or wobbles when they lean on it at standing height, but it feels solid when the desk is lowered to sitting height. The wobble usually shows up a few weeks after assembly, once the frame has settled under regular use.

## Before you start

Get the order number first. Ask whether the desk sits on carpet, hardwood, or an uneven floor; that answer changes which step matters most. A 4 mm hex key ships in the hardware bag, and any standard hex key set from a hardware store works if it's been lost.

## Steps

1. Lower the desk fully and unplug it before touching any hardware.
2. Find the crossbar under the desktop, the bar running between the two leg columns, and locate the four bolts holding it to the leg brackets, two per side.
3. Tighten the bolts in an X pattern: top left, then bottom right, then top right, then bottom left. Snug each one, then add a quarter turn past snug. Do not go past that.
4. Raise the desk to standing height and press down on each corner in turn. A wobble that's gone means the crossbar was the cause.
5. If the wobble is still there, check the leveling feet at the base of each leg by turning each one by hand. A foot that won't thread further, or is missing, is the source; order a replacement set (LS-ACC-011, $15.00) rather than mixing an old foot with new ones, since worn threads bind unevenly against fresh ones.
6. With the feet adjusted or replaced, set a level across the desktop side to side. Turn the feet under the low corner until it reads level, then recheck the wobble by hand.
7. If the desk still rocks after steps 3 through 6, put a level on the floor itself where the desk stands. A floor that's out of level by more than about a quarter inch across the footprint will wobble any desk no matter how tight the frame is.

## If the steps do not fix it

If the crossbar bolts were already tight, the feet are new, and the floor checks out level, the wobble is likely a bent leg column or a warped crossbar from transit. Open a ticket and request a frame inspection; if Priya or a senior agent confirms the bend, replace the desk under the standard 1-year warranty (effective 2024-01-15). Do not offer a partial refund in place of replacement. The warranty covers a full frame swap, not a discount on a desk that still wobbles.
