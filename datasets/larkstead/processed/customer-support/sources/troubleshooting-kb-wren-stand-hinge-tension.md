# Kb Wren Stand Hinge Tension

## Symptom

The stand's tilt hinge either won't hold the laptop at the angle the customer sets it to and drifts flat under the laptop's weight, or it's so stiff the customer has to use both hands to tilt it at all.

## Before you start

Have the order number ready. Ask the customer to flip the stand over; the tension screw sits in a small hex recess on the underside of the hinge, next to the embossed logo. A 2.5 mm hex key ships taped inside the box, and most eyeglass repair kits have one close enough in size if it's gone missing.

## Steps

1. For a hinge that drifts down under weight, turn the tension screw clockwise in eighth-turns, testing the hold after each turn by setting a laptop on the stand at a steep angle and letting go.
2. Stop turning once the stand holds the laptop steady at the steepest angle the customer actually uses day to day. Going tighter than that just makes routine tilting harder for no benefit.
3. For a hinge that's too stiff to move without both hands, turn the screw counterclockwise in eighth-turns instead, testing tilt effort by hand between turns.
4. If the screw turns freely with no resistance in either direction, the hinge has stripped internally and tension can't be restored by adjustment.

## If the steps do not fix it

A stripped hinge is a hardware fault, not a setup issue, and the fix is a replacement unit rather than a part, since the hinge isn't sold separately. Replace under the standard 1-year warranty (effective 2024-01-15) if the stand is still within a year of the order date. Check that date before promising anything; a stand ordered outside the window falls to a paid replacement instead.
