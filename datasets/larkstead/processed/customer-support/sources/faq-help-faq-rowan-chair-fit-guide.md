## Starting point by your height

Q: Where should I set the seat height before I fine-tune anything?
A: Start from your own height rather than guessing at the lever. If you're 5'2" to 5'6", set the seat around 16 to 17.5 inches. Between 5'7" and 5'11", aim for 17.5 to 19 inches. Above 6 feet, go to 19 to 21 inches, which is the top of the Rowan's adjustment range. These are starting points, not fixed numbers, so nudge from there once you're sitting in it.

Q: I'm right on the border between two ranges. Which way do I go?
A: Split the difference and adjust from there over a few days. Most people settle within an inch of wherever they started once the desk height is dialed in too.

## Pairing the chair with your desk

Q: Once the chair's set, how do I set the Birch desk to match?
A: Sit down, get the chair right first, then raise or lower the desk until your forearms sit flat on the surface with your elbows near 90 degrees. For someone in the 5'7" to 5'11" range that usually lands the desktop around 28 to 30 inches off the floor; taller and shorter users will land outside that band, and that's expected, not a sign something's off.

Q: I stand at the desk part of the day too. Does the seat height still matter?
A: The seat itself doesn't need to change for standing use since you're out of it, but when you drop back down to sit, whatever height you set following the ranges above is still the right one to return to.

## Tilt and armrests

Q: What should I do with the tilt tension knob?
A: Clockwise for more resistance if the seat back falls backward too easily, counterclockwise for less. Heavier users generally want more tension, lighter users less. There's no single right number here.

Q: How high should the armrests sit?
A: Level with your elbows when your shoulders are relaxed and your arms hang naturally, not pushed up so your shoulders shrug toward your ears. If your forearms rest on the desk more than on the armrests, lower the armrests out of the way instead of leaving them mid-air under the desktop.
