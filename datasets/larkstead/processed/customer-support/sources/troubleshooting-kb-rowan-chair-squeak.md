# Kb Rowan Chair Squeak

## Symptom

The chair makes a squeak or creak, usually when the customer leans back, swivels, or shifts weight side to side. Some describe one squeak per motion, others a steady creak under sustained recline.

## Before you start

Ask for the order number. Ask whether the squeak happens on recline, on swivel, or both; that answer points to a different bolt before the customer touches anything.

## Steps

1. Squeak on recline only: the tilt-tension knob under the seat front is loose. Turn it clockwise in quarter turns and test recline after each one until the squeak stops.
2. Squeak on swivel only: check the caster stems where they meet the base. Have the customer tip the chair on its side and confirm each of the 5 casters seats fully in its socket. A caster sitting proud by even an eighth of an inch will grind on swivel.
3. Squeak on both: check the seat pivot bolts, the two bolts joining the seat pan to the tilt mechanism underneath. Snug them with the 5 mm hex key from the hardware bag, alternating side to side, a quarter turn each pass.
4. If the bolts are already tight and the squeak persists, check the felt washer between the armrest post and the seat frame on each side. It ships in place at the factory, so it's rare for it to be missing, but a squeak that appears only when the customer rests an elbow and shifts weight often traces back to this washer sitting off-center or having worked loose.
5. Re-seat the washer by hand so it sits flush, then retest the motion that caused the original squeak.

## If the steps do not fix it

A gas lift cylinder that creaks under load, rather than squeaks on a specific motion, is a different fault; note that distinction in the ticket instead of treating it as a bolt issue. If the cylinder is the source, the chair qualifies for a replacement cylinder under the standard 1-year warranty (effective 2024-01-15). We don't stock loose washers or bolts for outbound shipment on their own. If a customer needs one after the steps above, send it from Portland showroom stock at no charge rather than asking them to order a part that has no SKU.
