## Before you start

Q: Do I need two people to assemble the desk?
A: Yes, for the 60 and 72 inch frames. The steel legs and crossbar are heavy enough that one person tipping the frame upright alone risks pinched fingers or a scratched birch top, so grab a second pair of hands before you unpack it; the 48 inch frame is light enough for one person if you take it slow. Budget 25 to 35 minutes either way.

Q: What tools come in the box?
A: The Allen key and the leg bolts, washers, and cable clips you need are all in a small parts bag taped inside the base carton. Bring your own Phillips screwdriver for the control box bracket, which isn't included.

## Bolting the frame

Q: What order should I tighten the bolts in?
A: Start with the crossbar-to-leg bolts on one side, hand tighten both, then do the same on the other side before fully torquing anything. Cross the desk like tightening lug nuts on a wheel, left leg, right leg, left leg again, right leg again, so the frame pulls together evenly instead of racking sideways. Fully tighten only once the frame sits square on the floor.

Q: The frame feels wobbly after I tighten everything. What's wrong?
A: Loosen the four crossbar bolts a quarter turn, push the frame square by hand, then retighten in the crossing order above. A frame torqued down while slightly out of square stays wobbly no matter how tight the bolts get.

## First-run calibration

Q: Why does the desk ask me to calibrate on first power-up?
A: The motor needs to learn its own top and bottom travel limits before the memory height presets will work. Press and hold the down arrow on the control pad until the desk lowers fully and the display flashes, then release. That's it.

Q: Can I skip calibration?
A: No. Without it the desk still raises and lowers manually, but the 1, 2, and 3 memory buttons won't save or return to a height, and the anti-collision stop that halts the desk if it hits something overhead won't work correctly either.
