## Definition
**Operating:** Customers are expected to independently assemble products, often requiring the ability to stabilize pieces while using their hands for assembly tasks. Assembly Help refers to the support mechanisms or resources provided to assist customers during the product assembly process, including visual guides and step-by-step instructions to facilitate understanding and execution.

## Main Insights
- Assembly instructions generally presume that users can maneuver parts of the product with one hand while operating tools with the other.
- Social support, such as photo assembly steps provided by individuals like Priya Raman to Tessa Nakamura, can enhance the clarity and ease of the assembly process.
- Visual aids, such as photographs, serve as effective tools for improving understanding of assembly tasks, especially for complex products.

## Why It Compounds
The significance of Assembly Help extends beyond mere convenience; it shapes customer satisfaction and retention in the consumer products industry. When customers are provided with adequate support and resources for assembly, their likelihood of successfully completing the assembly process increases, leading to a more positive overall experience. Additionally, effective assembly assistance reduces frustration and the potential for product returns, thereby benefiting businesses by fostering loyalty and trust among consumers.
