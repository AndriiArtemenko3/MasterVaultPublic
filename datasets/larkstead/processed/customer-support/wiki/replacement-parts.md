## Definition
**Operating:** Replacement parts are specific components sold separately for customer support, allowing users to repair products without needing to replace the entire item. 

## Main Insights
- The replacement parts catalog is intentionally kept limited, including items such as a desk platform, chair, and a shelf for common repairs.
- Customers can typically order just the broken piece rather than the whole product, making repairs more cost-effective.
- Currently, six replacement parts are available for various items, such as the Birch motor control box ($89.00) and Birch leveling feet ($15.00).
- Replacement parts are manufactured to meet precise hardware dimensions, ensuring compatibility with existing products.

## Why It Compounds
The concept of replacement parts is essential in the customer support domain as it enhances customer satisfaction by promoting long-term product usability and reducing waste. By allowing customers to fix rather than replace items, businesses can foster sustainable practices and build loyalty, as users appreciate the cost-saving avenues and convenience of maintaining their purchases. Moreover, efficiently managing replacement parts can lead to reduced return rates and increased customer retention.
