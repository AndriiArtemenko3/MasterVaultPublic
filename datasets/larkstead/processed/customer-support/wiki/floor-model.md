## Definition
**Operating:** A floor model is a display product used in retail environments that is not for sale, typically employed to demonstrate features and usability to customers. These models are available for customers to experience first-hand, making them a vital part of the purchasing journey.

## Main Insights
- The Rowan chair displayed in the showroom serves as a floor model exclusively for demonstration purposes.
- Customers looking to purchase a new Rowan chair will have the item shipped from stock rather than buying the floor model.

## Why It Compounds
The concept of the floor model is essential in customer support and retail because it enhances the customer experience by allowing potential buyers to physically interact with a product. This interaction can lead to informed purchasing decisions and foster confidence in the product's quality. Additionally, separating the sales of floor models from regular stock can create a clearer inventory management system, preventing confusion regarding product availability and pricing.
