## Definition
**Operating:** Product quality is currently assessed through customer feedback and observable defects reported in merchandise. In the customer support domain, product quality refers to the overall standard of goods as perceived by customers, particularly in relation to defects or issues that affect usability and satisfaction.

## Main Insights
- Priya has acknowledged an issue with the warp effect present in the Alder mats, indicating a recognized defect that needs addressing.  
- A specific batch of Alder mats, including items owned by Beatrix, is reported to have an edge issue, highlighting a consistent quality problem across multiple customers.  
- Customer feedback plays a crucial role in identifying quality defects, which can inform improvements in future product iterations.  

## Why It Compounds
Product quality not only affects customer satisfaction but also has far-reaching implications for brand reputation and customer loyalty. When defects like the warp and edge issues in Alder mats arise, they can lead to increased returns, loss of confidence, and negative reviews from customers. This, in turn, can impact sales and the company's market position. Furthermore, addressing these issues swiftly through effective customer support can enhance client trust and ultimately improve the overall quality assurance process.
