## Theme

Hold response time steady while ticket volume climbs, and finish rolling out the policy changes from Q1 cleanly across every customer-facing surface. The team is not adding headcount this year, so the quarter's wins have to come from process and documentation, not staffing.

## Ranked goals

### 1. Close the stale-reference gap from the January policy changes
Success criteria: zero customer-facing pages or macros still quoting the old 30-day return window or the old 75.00 free-shipping threshold.
Key dependency: a full audit pass across the help center, FAQ pages, and Helprise macro library.
Blocking risk: the January rollout already produced at least one live customer complaint over a stale 75.00 reference (ticket-sl5-ticket-hd-2026-00219-06); the audit needs to be exhaustive, not spot-checked.

### 2. Hold first-response time under the prior year's worst-month figure
Success criteria: Q2 average first-response time stays at or below the 11-business-hour figure recorded in August 2025.
Key dependency: macro coverage for the highest-volume ticket categories, so agents are not composing free-text replies on routine issues.
Blocking risk: no headcount is being added this quarter, so any volume spike beyond last year's pattern has no staffing lever to absorb it.

### 3. Monitor the LOT-2025-14 mat-defect tail for re-failures
Success criteria: zero new tickets tagged lot-2025-14 after the replacement batch shipped, confirming the Verdant QC addendum is holding.
Key dependency: the tagging discipline set up during the incident stays in place so any recurrence is caught fast, not rediscovered from scratch.
Blocking risk: the November 2025 Verdant watch flag on a different SKU line means vendor quality is not fully resolved company-wide, even if this specific lot is quiet.

## What this rules out

No new support hire this quarter, regardless of how Q2 volume trends; that question is scoped to the separate hire-support-agent decision with an April review date. No new return or shipping policy changes this quarter. Q1's changes need to finish landing before layering another one on top.

## Time budget

Roughly 60% of process-improvement time on the stale-reference audit and macro cleanup, 25% on response-time monitoring and reporting, 15% held in reserve for whatever the LOT-2025-14 tail or the Verdant watch flag actually produces.
