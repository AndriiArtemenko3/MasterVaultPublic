## Definition
**Operating:** The reporting path refers to the established protocol within an organization that allows employees to report incidents of harassment directly to designated individuals, specifically Mara Voss or Ana Petrova. Reports can be made through various means, with a preference for email communication to ensure a written record.

## Main Insights
- Employees can report harassment incidents directly to Mara Voss or Ana Petrova.
- Reports may be submitted in person, via phone, or preferably through email.
- Email is the preferred method for reporting due to its ability to provide documentation.

## Why It Compounds
Understanding the reporting path is crucial for fostering a safe and responsive workplace environment. It empowers employees to voice concerns about harassment without fear of reprisal and ensures that incidents are addressed promptly and effectively. By facilitating clear communication channels, organizations can uphold their commitment to employee welfare and organizational integrity, thereby enhancing workplace morale and productivity.
