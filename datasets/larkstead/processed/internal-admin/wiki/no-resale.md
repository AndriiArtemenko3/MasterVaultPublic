## Definition
**Operating:** Purchases made through employee discount programs are strictly for personal use and may not be resold. The "No Resale" policy prohibits employees from selling or transferring items acquired at a discounted rate for any monetary benefit. This policy aims to ensure that employee benefits remain exclusive to staff members and their immediate families only.

## Main Insights
- Employees are allowed to purchase items using their employee rate solely for personal use or for members of their immediate household.
- Reselling or transferring items bought with employee discounts for profit is explicitly prohibited.
- This restriction is intended to maintain the integrity of employee discount programs and prevent misuse of corporate resources.

## Why It Compounds
The "No Resale" policy is crucial not only for protecting company assets and maintaining fair employee benefits but also for fostering a culture of trust and accountability within the organization. By ensuring that the items purchased at reduced rates remain within the intended personal usage framework, companies can better manage inventory, enforce compliance with guidelines, and create a more sustainable employee benefits structure for everyone involved.
