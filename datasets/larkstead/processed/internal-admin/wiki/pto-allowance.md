## Definition
**Operating:** Every employee accrues Paid Time Off (PTO) at a rate of 15 days per calendar year, beginning immediately upon employment. This accrual is calculated at a rate of 1.25 days per month and is applicable to both full-time and part-time employees, with part-time staff accruing PTO proportionally based on their scheduled hours relative to a standard 40-hour work week.

## Main Insights
- Employees receive 15 days of paid time off annually.
- PTO accrual occurs at a rate of 1.25 days per month.
- Part-time employees earn PTO at the same rate, adjusted for their working hours.
- Accrual begins from the first day of employment, allowing immediate access to benefits.

## Why It Compounds
PTO allowance is crucial for promoting employee well-being and work-life balance. By ensuring that every employee, regardless of full-time or part-time status, can accrue leave starting from day one, organizations foster a culture of responsibility and care. This not only enhances employee satisfaction but also helps in retention, reducing turnover and recruitment costs. Furthermore, by allowing employees to take time off, businesses can enhance overall productivity as rested and recharged employees tend to perform better.
