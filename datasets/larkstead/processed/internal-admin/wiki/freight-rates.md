## Definition
**Operating:** Freight rates are the charges associated with the transportation of goods by various carriers, which can vary based on distance, type of shipment, and other factors. These rates determine the overall cost of logistics and are critical in supply chain management.

## Main Insights
- The cost for container drayage is billed at a flat rate of $1,850.00 per 40-foot container.
- Dwell charges apply for time beyond 48 hours, billed at $85.00 for each additional day.
- LTL (Less Than Truckload) shipments are charged per pallet based on mileage bands; for example, Band 1 (0-150 miles) is $2.10 per pallet-mile with a minimum fee of $175.00.
- There are four defined mileage bands with decreasing rates per pallet-mile as distance increases: Band 2 (151-350 miles) at $1.65, Band 3 (351-600 miles) at $1.20, and Band 4 (601+ miles) at $0.95.

## Why It Compounds
Understanding freight rates is essential for businesses that rely on shipping goods, as they can significantly affect operating costs, pricing strategies, and overall profitability. Businesses must be aware of these rates to negotiate better terms with carriers, optimize their shipping methods, and maintain competitive pricing in the market. Moreover, fluctuating freight rates can indicate changes in demand and supply within the logistics industry, affecting broader economic conditions.
