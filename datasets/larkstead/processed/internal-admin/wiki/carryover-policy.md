## Definition
**Operating:** Unused Paid Time Off (PTO) days can carry over into the next calendar year, limited to a maximum of 5 days. This policy allows employees to retain a portion of their PTO balance instead of losing it entirely at the end of the calendar year, thereby providing flexibility for vacation planning. The carried over PTO days will expire on March 31 of the following year if not used.

## Main Insights
- Unused PTO days can carry over into the next year, allowing employees to retain up to 5 days of leave instead of losing it.  
- Any carried-over PTO days must be utilized by March 31 of the following year, after which they will expire.  
- This policy aims to encourage employees to take time off while also recognizing that not everyone may be able to use all their allocated days within the calendar year.

## Why It Compounds
The carryover policy matters as it not only provides employees with additional flexibility in managing their time off but also promotes a healthier work-life balance. By allowing unused PTO days to roll over, organizations can reduce employee burnout and enhance job satisfaction, leading to improved productivity and retention in the long term. This approach can ultimately foster a workplace culture that values employee well-being and supports overall organizational health.
