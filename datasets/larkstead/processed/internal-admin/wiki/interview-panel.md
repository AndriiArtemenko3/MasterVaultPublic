## Definition
**Operating:** Every open role at our organization requires an interview panel consisting of at least two members: the hiring manager and a peer from a different function. This structured approach ensures diverse perspectives in the hiring process, promoting a fair evaluation of candidates across various departments.

## Main Insights
- Interview panels must include a minimum of two members: the hiring manager and a peer from a different function.
- For every hire, regardless of level, Mara is present during the final round of interviews to lend her expertise and judgment.

## Why It Compounds
The interview panel concept is crucial as it injects multiple viewpoints into the hiring process, reducing bias and enhancing decision-making quality. By involving employees from different functions, the organization can better evaluate how candidates will integrate into the company's culture and collaborate across teams. Mara's involvement in final interviews further ensures a level of consistency and rigor in candidate assessment, ultimately improving hiring outcomes.
