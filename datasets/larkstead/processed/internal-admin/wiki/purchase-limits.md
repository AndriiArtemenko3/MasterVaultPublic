## Definition
**Operating:** Employees can purchase two large items per rolling 12-month period. This practice ensures that the acquisition of high-value items is managed effectively, promoting responsible resource allocation within the organization.

## Main Insights
- Employees are limited to two large items within any rolling 12-month timeframe, helping to control costs and manage inventory effectively.
- The rolling cap resets based on the date of each purchase, allowing employees to make purchases spaced out over time rather than being restricted by a fixed calendar year.
- Accessories, including the Wren stand, Heron arms, and Vireo lamp, do not count against this purchase cap, facilitating the acquisition of supplementary items without penalizing the main purchases.

## Why It Compounds
Understanding purchase limits is crucial for internal administrative processes as it impacts budgeting, inventory management, and employee resource allocation. By setting a cap on large item purchases, organizations can better maintain fiscal health and ensure that resources are used in a manner that aligns with operational goals. This approach also encourages employees to prioritize their purchases and make informed decisions.
