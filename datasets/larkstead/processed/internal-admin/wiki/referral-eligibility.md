## Definition
**Operating:** Referral eligibility refers to the criteria and procedures that must be followed for an employee to be entitled to receive a referral bonus for recommending a candidate for a position within the organization. Referrals must be officially submitted via email to the designated contact before the candidate's first interview.

## Main Insights
- Referrals must be emailed to Ana with the referring employee's name included in the subject line.
- If a candidate has already applied independently, they are not eligible for a referral bonus even if highlighted after the fact.
- Ana and Mara oversee the hiring process but are excluded from receiving any referral bonuses.

## Why It Compounds
Understanding referral eligibility is crucial as it ensures a streamlined hiring process while fostering a culture of employee engagement in recruitment. By adhering to these guidelines, employees are incentivized to recommend potential hires who they believe will contribute positively to the organization, thus ultimately enhancing team dynamics and performance. Furthermore, clarity around the rules helps prevent confusion and manages expectations among staff, ensuring that referral practices align with organizational goals.
