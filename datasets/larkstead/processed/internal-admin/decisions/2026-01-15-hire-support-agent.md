## Question

Should Larkstead add a third support agent rather than holding the team at its current headcount through 2026?

## Options

**Option A: Hold support headcount flat and manage volume through macros and process fixes alone.**
Cheapest option and matches the stated 2026 priority of growing B2B without adding headcount, but response time is already drifting on peak months.

**Option B: Add a third support agent now.**
Fixes the response-time problem directly, but runs against the explicit no-headcount-growth priority set for the year.

**Option C: Add a part-time or seasonal agent scoped to peak months only.**
Splits the difference: addresses the measured peak-volume problem without a permanent headcount commitment.

## Evidence

- The stated first priority for 2026 is to grow B2B without increasing headcount (memo-org-memo-org-2026-planning-priorities-02); the third priority is to hold response times steady as ticket volume climbs (memo-org-memo-org-2026-planning-priorities-07). These two priorities are already in tension on their own terms.
- The last support hire was Celeste, added in March 2025 (memo-org-memo-org-2026-planning-priorities-08, memo-org-memo-org-support-headcount-celeste-ae-role-01); she was independently handling tickets solo within a week (memo-org-memo-org-support-headcount-celeste-ae-role-03).
- August 2025 CSAT data shows the strain: average first response time ran 11 business hours against high ticket volume that month (csat-batch-csat-2025-08-61245-11hr-response-06, csat-batch-csat-2025-08-61245-11hr-response-07).
- Holiday volume already pushes first response to roughly 9 business hours across November and December in a normal year (csat-batch-csat-2024-q4-61209-holiday-slowdown-05), meaning the August spike was not even the seasonal peak case.
- The team does hold a hard SLA in at least one lane: B2B tickets carry a 4-business-hour acknowledgment target (macro-macro-b2b-01-routing-04), a bar the general queue's 9-to-11-hour response times are nowhere near meeting for non-B2B tickets.
- August's CSAT score held at 4.2 of 5 despite the slow response time (csat-batch-csat-2025-08-61245-11hr-response-05), meaning the strain has not yet visibly hurt satisfaction, which weakens the urgency case but does not remove it.

## Criteria

Whether response-time drift is trending worse quarter over quarter, whether it is currently costing measurable CSAT or churn, and whether a seasonal hire can be scoped tightly enough to respect the no-headcount-growth priority in the months that matter least.

## Recommendation

No firm recommendation yet; this decision stays open pending one more quarter of response-time data. Option C is the most likely landing point: it respects the stated 2026 priority on permanent headcount while giving the team peak-season coverage where the CSAT data already shows strain.

## What would change my mind

A CSAT score drop below 4.0, or a churn-risk account naming slow support response as a reason to leave, either of which would override the no-headcount-growth priority outright.

## Next action

Pull Q2 2026 CSAT and first-response data before the 2026-04-15 review date and compare against the August 2025 and Q4 2024 baselines already on file.
