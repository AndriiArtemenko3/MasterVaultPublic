## Theme

Keep compliance and headcount cost flat while the support and sales teams both carry rising load without new hires. This is a bridge quarter: two named review dates fall in the second half of the year, and the job now is to walk into both with clean data rather than scrambling in July.

## Ranked goals

### 1. Prepare the Helprise data-retention review ahead of 2026-08-09
Success criteria: the photo-attachment retention gap (attachments do not purge on the same clock as ticket threads) is either fixed or explicitly scoped as a renewal-negotiation item before the review date.
Key dependency: a written position on Option A versus Option B from the open helprise-renewal decision, ready before the review conversation starts.
Blocking risk: the review date is fixed and does not move; if the position isn't ready by early August, the review happens on the vendor's terms instead of Larkstead's.

### 2. Hold the no-new-headcount line, but keep the support review honest
Success criteria: no permanent hires added in Q2 outside of previously committed roles, while the April 2026 hire-support-agent review runs on real Q1 data rather than being deferred again.
Key dependency: Q1 2026 CSAT and first-response numbers need to be pulled and compared against the August 2025 and Q4 2024 baselines already on file, not treated as a formality.
Blocking risk: the 2026 priority to grow B2B without increasing headcount is explicit and company-wide; the support review has real tension with it built in, and deferring the review again just pushes the tension into Q3.

### 3. Keep vendor-claim recordkeeping current
Success criteria: any new vendor defect claim this quarter follows the same documentation pattern used on the Verdant mat-defect claim: dated claim letter, itemized credit demand, signed response on file.
Key dependency: finance and operations stay aligned on who owns the paper trail when a claim spans both functions, as the Verdant claim did.
Blocking risk: the precedent exists in one incident only; without a written checklist, the next claim risks skipping a step the Verdant claim happened to get right by coincidence rather than by process.

## What this rules out

No proactive vendor search for a Helprise alternative this quarter; nothing in current evidence names a specific competitor worth evaluating, so that stays a placeholder option on the open decision rather than active work. No headcount additions outside the two already-scoped reviews: support hire, and any fallout from the Helprise review. This is not the quarter for a broader org-chart refresh.

## Time budget

Roughly 40% on Helprise review preparation, 35% on pulling and validating the Q1 2026 support data for the April review, 15% on vendor-claim process documentation, 10% in reserve.
