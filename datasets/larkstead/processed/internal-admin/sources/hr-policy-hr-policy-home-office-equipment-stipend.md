## Content

Policy: Home-office equipment stipend
Effective: 2026-02-16
Supersedes: none
Approved by: Mara Voss
Prepared by: Ana Petrova
Applies to: employees approved for regular remote work under the remote and hybrid work policy; warehouse staff are not covered, since that role carries no remote allowance; contractors are not covered

1. Amount
Each eligible employee receives a one-time stipend of 400.00 toward home-office equipment. This is a single payment per employee, not an annual allowance, and it is not prorated for part-time staff; the amount is the same 400.00 whether the employee works 2 remote days a week or 4.

2. Eligible purchases
The stipend covers desks, chairs, monitor arms, lighting, and small ergonomic accessories for the employee's home workspace. Larkstead does not require the purchase to come from its own catalog, though an employee buying a Rowan chair or a Heron monitor arm through the usual order flow can apply the stipend against that order instead of paying out of pocket and submitting a receipt after the fact.

3. Receipts
Receipts go to Ana within 60 days of the purchase, itemized enough to show what was bought and the amount paid. A stipend request with no receipt, or a receipt that just says "office supplies" with no breakdown, gets kicked back for detail before it is reimbursed. Purchases made more than 90 days before this policy's effective date are not eligible, even with a valid receipt.

4. Approval and payment
Because 400.00 falls under the 500.00 self-approve threshold in the standing expense policy, Ana processes the reimbursement directly once the receipt clears review; it does not need manager or Mara sign-off the way a larger expense would. Reimbursement lands in the employee's next regular pay cycle after the receipt is approved, not as a separate off-cycle payment.

5. Timing for newly remote staff
An employee who becomes eligible for remote work after this policy's effective date, whether through a new hire offer or a change in an existing role, has 90 days from the date remote status starts to make the purchase and submit the receipt. The stipend does not carry forward past that window, and an employee who lets it lapse does not get a second chance at it later without a new policy change.
