## Content

Policy: Contractor engagement
Effective: 2024-04-01
Supersedes: none
Approved by: Mara Voss
Prepared by: Ana Petrova
Applies to: all independent contractors engaged by Larkstead outside the vendor list

1. Statement of work required
Every contractor engagement starts with a written statement of work covering the deliverables, the days or hours committed per week, the rate, and an end date or renewal point. No contractor begins billable work before Mara signs the SOW, and verbal agreements to "start now, paper it later" are not honored for payment.

2. Fixed-day pattern
Contractors working an ongoing weekly pattern, rather than a single bounded project, commit to specific fixed days instead of a floating pool of hours that drifts week to week. The current live example is the Shopstack development contract, run at 2 fixed days per week, Tuesday and Thursday, with the contractor reachable only on those days per the SOW terms. Support and sales should plan Shopstack-related requests around that window rather than expecting same-day turnaround on a Wednesday.

3. Invoicing cadence
Contractors invoice monthly, in arrears, for the days actually worked that month. Invoices go to Ana with the SOW reference and a day-by-day breakdown of hours or fixed-day counts; she reconciles the invoice against the SOW before approving payment, and a mismatch gets kicked back rather than paid and corrected after the fact.

4. Approval thresholds
Any SOW or single invoice total reaching 250.00 needs manager-level sign-off. Totals at or above 1000.00 need Mara's approval, matching the standing expense policy thresholds already in force. Ana flags any invoice that runs over the SOW's committed rate before it goes out for payment, no exceptions.

5. No employee benefits
Contractors are not employees. They do not accrue PTO, are not on the payroll calendar, and are not covered under the fixed holiday schedule. A contractor whose fixed day happens to land on a company holiday simply does not invoice for that day unless the SOW states otherwise in writing.
