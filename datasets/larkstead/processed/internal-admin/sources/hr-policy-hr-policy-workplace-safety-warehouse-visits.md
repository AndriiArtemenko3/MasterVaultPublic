## Content

Policy: Workplace safety for warehouse and backroom visits
Effective: 2025-07-21
Supersedes: none
Approved by: Mara Voss
Prepared by: Ana Petrova
Applies to: all office-based staff, full and part time, who visit the Portland backroom or the ParcelPoint site in Reno; contractors on Larkstead business follow the same rules as employees; warehouse staff follow Hank Morrow's floor rules directly and are not the subject of this document

1. Scope
This policy covers office staff, meaning sales, marketing, finance, and support, who step into the packing and staging backroom at Larkstead's Portland space, or who travel to the ParcelPoint fulfillment center in Reno, Nevada, for a vendor visit or a quality check.

2. Backroom visits
An office employee may enter the Portland backroom to pull a sample, check inventory, or shadow a pack station. Closed-toe shoes are required, and the employee checks in with Hank Morrow or whoever is running the floor before stepping past the doorway. No one walks behind an active pack station without saying so out loud first. Box cutters stay in the hand of whoever is using them, never left open on a table or a cart.

3. Reno site visits
A visit to the ParcelPoint warehouse needs at least 3 business days of notice to Hank, who lines up the window with ParcelPoint's site contact. Closed-toe shoes and a hi-vis vest are required; ParcelPoint provides the vest at the door. No unaccompanied visits. A ParcelPoint staff member or Hank stays with the visitor for the full time on the floor, and forklift lanes are off-limits to office staff regardless of who is escorting.

4. Incident reporting
Any injury, near miss, or property damage during a visit gets reported to Hank and Ana within 24 hours, starting from whichever of the two the employee reaches first. A verbal report the same day is fine as long as a short written follow-up, even a couple of lines by email, lands inside that 24-hour window. Ana logs it against the Nehalem Mutual coverage if it looks like it could turn into a claim; Hank logs anything that points to a fix on the floor itself.

5. Non-compliance
Skip the required footwear or the escort and backroom or warehouse access is suspended until Hank signs off on it again.
