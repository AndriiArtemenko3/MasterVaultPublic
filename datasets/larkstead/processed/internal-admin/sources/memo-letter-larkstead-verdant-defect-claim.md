# Letter Larkstead Verdant Defect Claim

## Summary

To: Verdant Textiles Co. (VEN-01), attn.

## Content

To: Verdant Textiles Co. (VEN-01), attn. Lan Pham
From: Mara Voss, Founder & CEO, Larkstead Goods Company, LLC
Date: 2025-08-21
Subject: Defect claim, LOT-2025-14, INV-VRD-2025-118

Lan, we have a confirmed defect in LOT-2025-14. 127 of the 1400 units are
defective, 9.07% of the lot. Dmitri Okafor ran the numbers: 109 units
culled at our warehouse against an edge-stitch delamination criterion,
plus 18 confirmed field failures across 14 [[support]] tickets. The
failure mode is the same across every unit, the edge binding separates
from the mat core and the edge lifts, worse with humidity.

INV-VRD-2025-118 totals 18060.00, 1400 units at 12.90, dated 2025-06-24,
Net-45, due 2025-08-08. We are holding payment on this invoice pending
resolution of this claim.

We are asking for a credit of 1841.50. That breaks into 1638.30 for the
127 defective units at the 12.90 invoice price, plus 203.20 in pro-rated
inbound freight and receiving on those same 127 units, at 1.60 per unit.
The freight figure reflects our actual landed cost on the defective
units, not just the goods price. We think that's fair given the units
never should have shipped.

We need a corrective action plan within 10 business days. That plan
should cover what went wrong in production, what changes at your end so
this doesn't happen again on the next lot, and any inspection step you
can add before shipment. We are not asking you to guess at causes, we
want the actual production record for this lot.

We value the relationship and want this resolved cleanly, not as a
one-off fight over 1841.50. When can we expect the corrective action
plan?

Mara Voss
Founder & CEO, Larkstead Goods Company, LLC
