## Content

Policy: Hiring and referral
Effective: 2025-04-07
Supersedes: none
Approved by: Mara Voss
Prepared by: Ana Petrova
Applies to: all current employees, full and part time, for referral eligibility; the interview process applies to every open role, whether the hire will be an employee or a contractor

1. Referral bonus
A current employee who refers a candidate that Larkstead goes on to hire receives a 500.00 bonus. Payment lands on the payroll run following the new hire's 90th day, provided both the new hire and the referring employee are still with Larkstead on that date. If either one has left by day 90, the bonus is not paid, no partial amount, no later reconsideration.

2. Eligibility and submission
Referrals go to Ana by email, resume attached, with the referring employee's name in the subject line, before the candidate's first interview. A candidate who already applied on their own does not count as a referral even if a coworker mentions them afterward. Mara and Ana run the hiring process, so neither one is eligible to earn a referral bonus themselves.

3. Interview panel
Every open role gets a panel of at least two people: the hiring manager for that role, plus one peer from a different function who can judge fit outside the immediate team. Mara sits in on the final round for every hire, at every level, since there is no separate HR function to delegate that step to. For a support-role hire, for instance, the panel might run Priya Raman with Dmitri Okafor added for a cross-functional read, then Mara for the close.

4. Reference checks
Two reference checks, phone or email, happen before an offer goes out. Ana or the hiring manager runs them and logs a short note from each call in the candidate's file.

5. Offer approval
A starting salary or a contractor rate is proposed by the hiring manager and signed off by Mara before anything is extended. Nothing goes out verbally, and nothing goes out in writing, ahead of that signoff.
