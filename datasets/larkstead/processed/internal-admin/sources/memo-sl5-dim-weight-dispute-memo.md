## Content

To: Mara Voss
From: Ana Petrova
Date: 2026-03-10
Subject: [APPROVAL] ParcelPoint dimensional weight dispute, 2392.50

ParcelPoint has billed a dimensional weight adjustment on heavy desk and chair cartons for two invoices running, and it isn't allowed under the contract. Total in dispute: 2392.50, made up of 1320.00 on INV-PPF-2026-002 and 1072.50 on INV-PPF-2026-003.

Exhibit B of the 2025-07-28 amendment is specific on this: heavy weight-class cartons bill at actual scale weight, full stop, and the dimensional-weight calculation (divisor 166) applies only to light and medium parcels. ParcelPoint applied a divisor of 139 instead, to 96 January cartons and 78 February cartons, both heavy desk and chair shipments, at 13.75 per carton. A lower divisor produces a higher billed dimensional weight for the same box, which is exactly backward from what heavy cartons should ever see under Exhibit B.

I short-paid INV-PPF-2026-002 by the disputed 1320.00 on 2026-03-06, ahead of its 2026-03-07 due date, paying 17894.30 instead of the full 19214.30. That call was mine and it's above my 999.99 approval band, so I'm asking you to ratify it after the fact rather than before, given the due date was the next day.

If ParcelPoint credits the full 2392.50 by 2026-03-31, INV-PPF-2026-003 pays on its due date as normal. If not, I'll hold that one too and escalate to Carla Munoz directly.

Can you sign off on the short-pay by end of week?

Ana
