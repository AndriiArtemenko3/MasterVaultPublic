# Template: competitor note (Pipewell knowledge card)

Doc class: SALES-CRM. Internal reference card on one competitor, kept in
Pipewell and revised when new intel arrives. Read with `../company.yaml`
and `../style-rules.md`. The only named competitor in this universe is
ErgoNest; do not invent others without a bible change.

## Skeleton

```
Competitor: <name>
Card revised: <YYYY-MM-DD> by <initials>
Prior revision: <YYYY-MM-DD> | none
Sources: <how we know: their site (date checked), customer-shared quote
(account, date), lost-deal debrief (PW-slug)>

## Positioning

<2-3 sentences, factual.>

## Price posture

<observed relationship to our list, with dated examples.>

## Where they win

- <honest, specific>

## Where we win

- <honest, specific>

## Ground rules

<what reps say and never say about them.>
```

## Exemplar (half length)

```
Competitor: ErgoNest
Card revised: 2026-03-04 by TA
Prior revision: 2025-10-20
Sources: ergonest site checked 2026-03-02; quote shared by Foxglove
Studios 2026-02-25; lease terms as reported at Kestrel's showroom
walk-in 2025-12-09 (PW-kestrel-10seat).

## Positioning

Direct-import reseller of white-label ergonomic desks and chairs. Sells
on price and shipping speed. Added a per-seat B2B leasing program in
late 2025; two of our accounts have now been offered it.

## Price posture

10-18% below our list on comparable desks. Discount codes most weeks.
The Foxglove quote (2026-02-25) came in 14% under our 26-seat figure.

## Where they win

- lower sticker, and the lease removes the capital ask
- 2-day shipping across the western US
- 9 chair models to our 1

## Where we win

- replacement parts catalog; they have none, a broken caster means a new chair
- 45-day returns against their 14
- in-house support; theirs is outsourced with slow escalation
- their smart lamp has no update path after purchase

## Ground rules

Compare terms, never speculate about their finances or quality we have
not seen. Nothing in writing to a customer that names them; comparisons
in customer-facing docs stay unnamed ("other direct-import desks").
```

## Realism notes

- Every claim carries a source and a date. "Their site checked 2026-03-02"
  ages honestly; an undated claim cannot be trusted or contradicted later.
- Where-they-win is as specific as where-we-win. A card that only lists
  their weaknesses is a pep talk, not intel.
- The card is revised, not rewritten: the Prior revision line plus fresh
  dated sources shows accretion. Every source date must sit on or before
  the Card revised date; intel heard later waits for the next pass. A card
  citing an event after its own revision date is a defect.
- Ground rules encode real sales-floor discipline: no named competitor in
  customer-facing paper. Corpus proposals and case studies must obey this.
- Figures cohere with company.yaml (10-18% posture, 14-day window, leasing
  from late 2025) and with storyline events that reference them.
