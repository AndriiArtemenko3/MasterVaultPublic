# Template: sales call transcript (Pipewell call recording)

Doc class: SALES-CRM. Auto-transcribed recording attached to a Pipewell
opportunity. Read with `../company.yaml` and `../style-rules.md`.

## Skeleton

```
Call: PW-<slug>, <YYYY-MM-DD>
Duration: <mm:ss>
Participants: <NAME (Larkstead)>, <NAME (account, role)>
Recorded via: Pipewell call recording; auto-transcript, lightly corrected

[00:00] NAME: <small talk opens the call: weather, scheduling, the office
move. 2-6 turns before business.>
[00:14] NAME: <turns carry disfluencies: "um", "uh", "you know", false
starts marked with a double dash: "we were-- I mean the second floor">
[01:03] NAME: [overlapping] <interruptions marked inline>
[01:05] NAME: <numbers in speech may be words and rounded; the paired
proposal carries the exact figures>
...
[mm:ss] NAME: <call ends on logistics or mid-thought; no summary turn>
[mm:ss] -- recording ends
```

Speaker labels are ALL-CAPS first names, consistent for the whole call.
Timestamps are `[mm:ss]` from recording start, strictly increasing.

Participants: TOM (Larkstead), DANA (Juniper Cowork, community manager)
Recorded via: Pipewell call recording; auto-transcript, lightly corrected

[00:18] DANA: The phasing, honestly. We can't take three floors of

## Realism notes

- Small talk opens every call, 2-6 turns, before anyone says a number.
  Cutting straight to business is the tell of a written scene.
- Disfluencies are load-bearing: "um", "uh", false starts with a double
  dash, [overlapping] interruptions, and one participant trailing off. About
  one disfluency every 3-4 turns; more reads like parody.
- Spoken numbers are words and get rounded ("roughly sixty-three two"). The
  exact arithmetic lives in the paired proposal, and the two must agree:
  55 x 1,149.00 = 63,195.00 list, 12% tier per the 2025-12-08 sheet.
- Calls end on logistics or mid-sentence, never on a recap. The transcript
  can cut off before the goodbye.
- Voice cards survive transcription: Tom's "fair enough?", first names,
  dollar figures early. The customer sounds like a customer, with their own
  concerns (freight elevators, board meetings) that go beyond the deal.
- Facts said aloud must match the version in force on the call date; a
  2026-04 call quotes the 12%-at-30 tier, not the retired 15%-at-25 one.
