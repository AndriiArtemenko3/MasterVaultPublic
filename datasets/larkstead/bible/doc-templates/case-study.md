# Template: customer case study (published)

Doc class: SALES-CRM. Published on the storefront and sent by sales as a PDF.
Marketing owns the copy. Read with `../company.yaml`, `../style-rules.md`,
and the storyline that owns the deal (SL3 for Cobalt Dental).

## Skeleton

```
Case study: <account name>
Published: <YYYY-MM-DD>
Written by: <staff full name>, approved by <account contact> <YYYY-MM-DD>

## Who

<2-3 sentences: the customer, their size, their constraint.>

## What they bought

<seat count and SKUs by name. Never prices paid, never discounts.>

## How it went

<rollout timeline with real dates and one concrete logistics fact.>

## Results

<2-4 measurable statements with their source and base. No superlatives.>

> "<approved quote in the contact's voice>"
> <name, role, account>

<one-line footer: contact for B2B inquiries.>
```

## Exemplar (half length)

```
Case study: Cobalt Dental Group
Published: 2026-03-10
Written by: Sofia Grieg, approved by Sam Ortiz 2026-03-06

## Who

Cobalt Dental Group runs four clinics around Portland. Front-office and
admin staff sat on mismatched furniture accumulated across three office
moves, and no two clinics could share parts.

## What they bought

40 seats of the Canopy team bundle: 60 in Birch standing desk, Rowan
task chair, Heron single monitor arm, Sparrow cable kit. Two Vireo desk
lamps for the two windowless back offices.

## How it went

Rollout ran December 2025 through February 2026 in four delivery waves,
one per clinic, the first on 2025-12-10. Each wave landed on a Wednesday,
the day each clinic blocks for admin, so assembly never cost patient
hours.

## Results

All four clinics live by 2026-02-20. Zero units damaged across four
freight deliveries. One parts catalog now covers every seat in the
group: a worn caster is a 24.00 fix, not a chair order.

> "Four clinics, one furniture standard. When something wears out we
> order the part and it's done."
> Sam Ortiz, office manager, Cobalt Dental Group

B2B inquiries: tom@larkstead.example
```

## Realism notes

- Prices paid, discounts, and margins never appear. The Cobalt deal
  carried a 15% tier plus a 3% exception; the case study says "40 seats"
  and stops. Deal economics live in Pipewell and Ledgerly docs only.
- Every fact must trace to the owning storyline: 40x LS-BDL-002 plus two
  Vireo lamps, four waves, first delivery 2025-12-10 are SL3 canon. The two
  lamps fall in the vireo-v12-affected warranty scope; marketing copy stays
  silent on that but must not contradict it.
- Results are countable and small: dates hit, zero damage, a 24.00 part.
  Fabricated productivity percentages are the fastest way to make a case
  study read like an ad.
- The quote sounds like the contact, is short, and carries an approval
  date before the publish date. Legal reality: no quote without sign-off.
- Marketing register per style-rules: short sentences, concrete numbers,
  no superlative chains, no closing zoom-out about the future of work.
- Sales sends this to prospects, so the corpus can show it attached in
  later threads (Beacon Legal cites Larkstead precedents in negotiation).
